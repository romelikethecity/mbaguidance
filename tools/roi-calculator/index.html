How We Calculate MBA ROI

The calculator accounts for:

  • Direct costs: Tuition, fees, and living expenses for the program duration, minus scholarships.
  • Opportunity cost: The salary you forgo while in school.
  • Salary uplift: The difference between your post-MBA salary and what you'd earn without the MBA.
  • Breakeven point: How many years of post-MBA salary uplift it takes to recoup your total investment.
  • 10-year net gain: Total additional earnings over 10 years after graduation, assuming 3% annual raises for both MBA and non-MBA paths.

This is a simplified model. Real-world factors like signing bonuses, equity compensation, career switches, and promotions can significantly improve MBA returns. See our full MBA ROI analysis for a deeper discussion.
