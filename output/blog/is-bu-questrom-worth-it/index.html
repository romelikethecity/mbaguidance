Is BU Questrom Worth It?

Honest ROI analysis for 2026

The Numbers

Ranking#32
Avg. Salary$140,000
Annual Tuition$62,160
Employment90%

The all-in cost of BU Questrom (tuition + living expenses + opportunity cost from an $80K salary) is approximately $344,320. The average starting salary of $140,000 produces an annual uplift of $60,000 over the $80K baseline. At that rate, you break even in approximately 5.7 years.

The breakeven calculation flatters or hurts BU Questrom depending on your pre-MBA salary. Coming from $60K? Breakeven drops to 4.3 years. Coming from $120K? Breakeven stretches to 17.2 years. The honest math: MBAs work best for career changers earning under the post-MBA median, not for high earners moving sideways.

What BU Questrom grads earn by industry

The $140,000 median masks meaningful spread by industry. Across all industries, BU Questrom's $140,000 median salary reflects a mix of consulting, finance, and tech placement. The salary range across roles is wider at BU Questrom than at industry-focused programs because career outcomes are more diverse.

BU Questrom's strongest placement industries are Healthcare, Technology, Social Impact. Salary distributions cluster around the mean for industries where the school recruits heavily, with a longer tail in industries where placements are rarer (and often more selective on the candidate side).

The 10-year financial picture

One-year salary comparisons miss the trajectory effect. A $140,000 starting salary at BU Questrom grows faster than an $80K salary without an MBA. By year 10, the cumulative income advantage from BU Questrom is approximately $600,000 before accounting for promotion velocity differences.

The trajectory difference is sharpest in consulting, finance, and tech, where MBA-track promotions to Manager, VP, and Principal levels happen 2-4 years faster than equivalent non-MBA paths. By year 5-7 post-MBA, the gap with the no-MBA counterfactual widens dramatically. The MBA's value is rarely captured in year-one salary comparisons.

When BU Questrom Is Worth It

  • Career changers targeting Healthcare, Technology, Social Impact: BU Questrom's recruiting pipelines in these areas are well-established. If you're pivoting from a lower-paying industry, the salary uplift is significant.
  • Candidates with scholarship funding: A $50K-$100K scholarship dramatically improves ROI, reducing the breakeven by 1-2 years. BU Questrom's ROI is already strong. Even without scholarship funding, the math works for most career changers. Scholarship offers above 25% of tuition push the breakeven below 3 years, which is exceptional.
  • Targeting roles that require the credential: In consulting, banking, and PE, the top-50 MBA credential is a prerequisite, not a nice-to-have. BU Questrom's #32 ranking qualifies.
  • Network-dependent careers: If your post-MBA path runs through PE, VC, or startup founding, the BU Questrom alumni network carries compounding returns for 20+ years that no spreadsheet captures.
  • Coming from an under-represented background: If you're a military veteran, non-profit operator, or career changer from a non-corporate field, the MBA is the most reliable way to credential into corporate America. BU Questrom's admissions team values these backgrounds.

When It Might Not Be

  • Already earning $120,000+ in your target industry: If you're already near the post-MBA salary, the ROI depends on career acceleration rather than immediate salary uplift.
  • Taking on full debt at $124,320+ in tuition alone: High debt loads narrow your post-MBA choices. You may feel pressured to take the highest-paying offer rather than the best career fit.
  • Targeting industries where the MBA credential is optional: In entrepreneurship, some tech roles, and creative industries, the MBA provides network but not credential value. The ROI calculation shifts toward intangibles.
  • Going to business school to figure out your career: BU Questrom is a $400K+ way to find clarity. Career coaching, informational interviews, and structured self-reflection cost a fraction of an MBA and produce equivalent clarity.

Scholarship math at BU Questrom

Scholarships shift the ROI calculation more than any other variable. BU Questrom's ROI is already strong. Even without scholarship funding, the math works for most career changers. Scholarship offers above 25% of tuition push the breakeven below 3 years, which is exceptional.

The negotiation playbook: collect competing offers from peer schools, communicate them politely to BU Questrom's admissions or financial aid office, and ask if BU Questrom can match or exceed. Schools at BU Questrom's ranking tier expect this conversation. A polite, evidence-based ask often yields $20K-$50K in additional funding. The worst outcome is they say no.

The Verdict

“BU Questrom can be worth it for the right candidate. The key is whether the school's strengths in Healthcare, Technology, Social Impact align with your career goals and whether you can manage the cost through scholarships or in-state tuition. The $140,000 average salary produces an acceptable ROI for career changers coming from lower-paying roles.”
