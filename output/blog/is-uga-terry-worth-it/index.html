Is UGA Terry Worth It?

Honest ROI analysis for 2026

The Numbers

The all-in cost of UGA Terry (tuition + living expenses + opportunity cost from an $80K salary) is approximately $247,836. The average starting salary of $119,000 produces an annual uplift of $39,000 over the $80K baseline. At that rate, you break even in approximately 6.4 years.

The breakeven calculation flatters or hurts UGA Terry depending on your pre-MBA salary. Coming from $60K? Breakeven drops to 4.2 years. Coming from $120K? Breakeven stretches to 247836.0 years. The honest math: MBAs work best for career changers earning under the post-MBA median, not for high earners moving sideways.

What UGA Terry grads earn by industry

The $119,000 median masks meaningful spread by industry. At MBB firms (McKinsey, BCG, Bain), UGA Terry grads typically clear $235K-$255K in first-year total compensation. Tier 2 strategy firms add $190K-$220K. UGA Terry's #51 ranking puts it in the recruiting target list for these firms.

UGA Terry's strongest placement industries are Consulting, Healthcare, Value Seekers. Salary distributions cluster around the mean for industries where the school recruits heavily, with a longer tail in industries where placements are rarer (and often more selective on the candidate side).

The 10-year financial picture

One-year salary comparisons miss the trajectory effect. A $119,000 starting salary at UGA Terry grows faster than an $80K salary without an MBA. By year 10, the cumulative income advantage from UGA Terry is approximately $390,000 before accounting for promotion velocity differences.

The trajectory difference is sharpest in consulting, finance, and tech, where MBA-track promotions to Manager, VP, and Principal levels happen 2-4 years faster than equivalent non-MBA paths. By year 5-7 post-MBA, the gap with the no-MBA counterfactual widens dramatically. The MBA's value is rarely captured in year-one salary comparisons.

When UGA Terry Is Worth It

  • Career changers targeting Consulting, Healthcare, Value Seekers: UGA Terry's recruiting pipelines in these areas are well-established. If you're pivoting from a lower-paying industry, the salary uplift is significant.
  • Candidates with scholarship funding: A $50K-$100K scholarship dramatically improves ROI, reducing the breakeven by 1-2 years. For UGA Terry to clear the breakeven inside 5 years from a $80K base, you'd need scholarship funding covering at least 95% of tuition. Negotiate aggressively or consider in-state alternatives.
  • Targeting roles that require the credential: In consulting, banking, and PE, the top-75 MBA credential is a prerequisite, not a nice-to-have. UGA Terry's #51 ranking qualifies.
  • Network-dependent careers: If your post-MBA path runs through PE, VC, or startup founding, the UGA Terry alumni network carries compounding returns for 20+ years that no spreadsheet captures.
  • Coming from an under-represented background: If you're a military veteran, non-profit operator, or career changer from a non-corporate field, the MBA is the most reliable way to credential into corporate America. UGA Terry's admissions team values these backgrounds.

When It Might Not Be

  • Already earning $99,000+ in your target industry: If you're already near the post-MBA salary, the ROI depends on career acceleration rather than immediate salary uplift.
  • Taking on full debt at $27,836+ in tuition alone: High debt loads narrow your post-MBA choices. You may feel pressured to take the highest-paying offer rather than the best career fit.
  • Targeting industries where the MBA credential is optional: In entrepreneurship, some tech roles, and creative industries, the MBA provides network but not credential value. The ROI calculation shifts toward intangibles.
  • Going to business school to figure out your career: UGA Terry is a $400K+ way to find clarity. Career coaching, informational interviews, and structured self-reflection cost a fraction of an MBA and produce equivalent clarity.

Scholarship math at UGA Terry

Scholarships shift the ROI calculation more than any other variable. For UGA Terry to clear the breakeven inside 5 years from a $80K base, you'd need scholarship funding covering at least 95% of tuition. Negotiate aggressively or consider in-state alternatives.

The negotiation playbook: collect competing offers from peer schools, communicate them politely to UGA Terry's admissions or financial aid office, and ask if UGA Terry can match or exceed. Schools at UGA Terry's ranking tier expect this conversation. A polite, evidence-based ask often yields $20K-$50K in additional funding. The worst outcome is they say no.

The Verdict

“UGA Terry can be worth it for the right candidate. The key is whether the school's strengths in Consulting, Healthcare, Value Seekers align with your career goals and whether you can manage the cost through scholarships or in-state tuition. The $119,000 average salary produces an acceptable ROI for career changers coming from lower-paying roles.”
