The Phoenix-Tempe MBA Job Market

Phoenix has attracted major corporate investment. Taiwan Semiconductor (TSMC) is building a multi-billion-dollar fab. Intel has a massive Chandler campus. JPMorgan Chase, American Express, and State Farm have major operations here. The metro's supply chain and semiconductor focus pairs well with ASU Carey's top-5 supply chain program.

Cost of Living

Moderate. Tempe rent runs $1,300-$1,900/month. Phoenix and Scottsdale are similar. Lower than coastal cities but rising. No state income tax phase-in has made Arizona more attractive for high earners. The heat is free but the A/C bills are real.

Why Get an MBA in Phoenix-Tempe

ASU Carey (#31) has a top-5 supply chain program and is rising quickly in overall rankings. Arizona Eller (#63) in Tucson provides a more traditional campus experience. For supply chain, semiconductor, or Southwest careers, Carey's trajectory and Phoenix's corporate investment make this a market to watch.

What to Watch Out For

Phoenix summers are extreme (115+ degrees). The city is car-dependent with limited public transit. Tucson is smaller and hotter. Arizona's MBA programs are regionally focused. For national consulting or banking careers, you'll recruit against geographic distance.

Frequently Asked Questions

What MBA programs are in Arizona?

ASU Carey (#31) in Tempe and Arizona Eller (#63) in Tucson. Carey is the significantly higher-ranked program, with a top-5 supply chain program and strong innovation curriculum.

Is Phoenix a growing job market?

Rapidly. TSMC's semiconductor investment, Intel's expansion, and corporate relocations from California are driving job growth. The supply chain and tech sectors are particularly strong. Phoenix is one of the fastest-growing metros in the US.

How hot does it get in Phoenix?

Brutal. June through September averages 100-115 degrees. October through April is pleasant (60-80s). If you can handle the summer heat, the other 7 months are some of the best weather in the country.
