The Salt Lake City MBA Job Market

Utah's Silicon Slopes tech corridor has become one of the fastest-growing tech markets in the US. Qualtrics, Pluralsight, Domo, and dozens of SaaS companies are headquartered here. Goldman Sachs has a major Salt Lake City office. Healthcare (Intermountain Health) and outdoor recreation companies (Black Diamond, Backcountry) add industry diversity.

Cost of Living

Moderate and rising. Salt Lake City rent runs $1,200-$1,800/month. Provo (near BYU) is cheaper at $900-$1,400. Both are well below coastal prices. No state income tax until recently (Utah has a flat income tax but it's lower than most).

Why Get an MBA in Salt Lake City

Two strong programs serve different markets. Utah Eccles (#87) in Salt Lake City provides access to Silicon Slopes tech companies. BYU Marriott (#39) in Provo is one of the best values in MBA education with exceptionally low tuition and a massive LDS business network. Both offer world-class outdoor access (skiing, hiking, climbing).

What to Watch Out For

Utah's culture is distinct. The LDS influence is real, especially at BYU and in Provo. If you're not LDS, make sure you visit and feel comfortable with the culture. Salt Lake City is more diverse and cosmopolitan than Provo. Air quality (winter inversions) can be an issue.

Frequently Asked Questions

What MBA programs are near Salt Lake City?

Utah Eccles (#87) is in Salt Lake City. BYU Marriott (#39) is 45 minutes south in Provo. BYU Marriott is the higher-ranked program with remarkably low tuition for its quality level.

Is Utah good for tech careers?

Yes and growing rapidly. Silicon Slopes (the Lehi-SLC corridor) hosts Qualtrics, Pluralsight, Domo, and hundreds of SaaS startups. Goldman Sachs has a major office here. The tech-to-cost-of-living ratio is one of the best in the country.

How affordable are Utah MBA programs?

BYU Marriott's tuition is among the lowest of any top-40 MBA program (approximately $15,000/year for LDS members, $30,000 for non-members). Utah Eccles offers competitive in-state rates. Combined with Utah's moderate cost of living, both programs offer exceptional ROI.
