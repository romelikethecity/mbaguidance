The Columbus MBA Job Market

Columbus is Ohio's economic engine and one of the fastest-growing Midwest cities. JPMorgan Chase has its credit card operations here (10,000+ employees). Nationwide Insurance, Cardinal Health, and L Brands (Victoria's Secret) are headquartered in the metro. A growing tech scene and Amazon's logistics operations add to the mix.

Cost of Living

Affordable. Rent runs $1,000-$1,600/month. Columbus is cheaper than most major metros while offering a surprisingly vibrant food, arts, and nightlife scene. The Ohio State campus area (including Short North and German Village) is particularly lively.

Why Get an MBA in Columbus

Ohio State Fisher (#34) provides a Big Ten MBA with direct access to Columbus's Fortune 500 employers. JPMorgan, Nationwide, and Cardinal Health recruit on campus. For candidates committed to the Midwest, Fisher's combination of corporate access and in-state affordability is compelling.

What to Watch Out For

Columbus is growing but still a secondary market. National recruiting from Fisher is possible but harder than from top-20 programs. If you want NYC, SF, or Chicago careers, consider whether Fisher's brand carries far enough. Ohio winters are cold but milder than Michigan or Minnesota.

Frequently Asked Questions

What MBA programs are in Columbus?

Ohio State Fisher (#34) is the city's MBA program. It's a well-regarded Big Ten program with strong local placement into JPMorgan, Nationwide, Cardinal Health, and other Columbus-area employers.

Is Columbus a good city for MBA graduates?

Growing and increasingly attractive. The city's corporate density, affordable living, and cultural scene make it one of the better Midwest cities for young professionals. The job market isn't as deep as Chicago or NYC, but it's strong and diversifying.

How much does Ohio State Fisher cost?

In-state tuition is approximately $30,000/year. Out-of-state is approximately $52,000/year. Combined with Columbus's moderate cost of living, Fisher offers strong value, especially for Ohio residents.
