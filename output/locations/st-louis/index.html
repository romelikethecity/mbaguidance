The St. Louis MBA Job Market

St. Louis anchors around healthcare (BJC HealthCare, Centene, Express Scripts), consumer goods (AB InBev North America, Purina, Energizer), and financial services (Edward Jones, Stifel). The metro hosts nine Fortune 500 companies. Boeing Defense has major operations here. Consulting firms (McKinsey, BCG, Deloitte) all have St. Louis offices that recruit from WashU.

Cost of Living

Exceptionally affordable. Rent runs $900-$1,400/month. St. Louis's cost of living is 20-25% below the national average. For an MBA at a private university like WashU, the affordable living costs significantly improve the ROI equation.

Why Get an MBA in St. Louis

WashU Olin (#27) is a top-30 MBA with one of the smallest class sizes in the country (130 students). Every student gets individualized career support. The small class creates deep relationships with peers and faculty. St. Louis's nine Fortune 500 companies provide local employer access, and Olin graduates recruit nationally into consulting, finance, and corporate roles.

What to Watch Out For

St. Louis is a secondary market. For careers in NYC, SF, or Chicago, you'll recruit from a distance. Olin's small class limits the alumni network breadth. The city has struggled with population loss and some neighborhoods have challenging safety reputations, though the WashU area (Clayton, Central West End) is upscale and safe.

Frequently Asked Questions

What MBA programs are in St. Louis?

WashU Olin (#27) is the city's MBA program. It's a top-30 nationally-ranked program at a prestigious private research university. The class size of 130 students makes it one of the smallest top-30 programs.

Is St. Louis affordable for MBA students?

Very. Cost of living is among the lowest of any city with a top-30 MBA program. Combined with scholarship opportunities from Olin, the total investment can be significantly lower than comparable programs in higher-cost cities.

Do WashU Olin graduates recruit nationally?

Yes. While many graduates take St. Louis-based roles (particularly in healthcare and consulting), a significant portion recruit into Chicago, NYC, and other major markets. Olin's career services team supports national job searches actively.
