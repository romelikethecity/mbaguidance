Program Overview

Orlando attracts 75 million visitors per year. Walt Disney World, Universal Studios, SeaWorld, and the convention industry create a hospitality economy that dwarfs most cities. UCF's MBA connects to that ecosystem with hospitality management, analytics, and supply chain concentrations. But the program isn't limited to tourism: Orlando's simulation and training industry (military and commercial) and a growing tech sector expand the career options.

At $13,500 per year for in-state students, UCF is one of the most affordable MBAs in Florida. The 33-student class gets a focused program in a university with 70,000+ students, which means extensive alumni connections across industries. UCF's downtown Orlando executive MBA campus serves working professionals. The program's value proposition is straightforward: Orlando employer access at rock-bottom tuition.

Culture & Community

UCF is the largest university in Florida by enrollment, and that scale creates a broad alumni base. The MBA class is small (33 students), which provides a tight cohort within a massive university. Orlando's year-round sunshine, theme parks, and affordable housing make quality of life a selling point. The student body mixes hospitality professionals, tech workers, and military-connected students from the nearby simulation and defense industry. The culture is practical and career-focused.

Academics & Curriculum

Hospitality management courses draw on Orlando's tourism infrastructure. Simulation and modeling expertise connects to the defense and training industry. Analytics and supply chain concentrations serve broader business careers. The core curriculum covers business fundamentals with flexibility to specialize. UCF's Institute for Simulation and Training is a unique academic resource. Faculty bridge tourism research and practical business applications.

Career Outcomes

Walt Disney World, Universal Studios, Lockheed Martin, and Deloitte are among the top regional employers. Hospitality management, consulting, finance, and technology roles drive placement. Average salary is approximately $78,000. Orlando's growth as a tech and startup hub is expanding the career options beyond tourism. Career services connects students to Central Florida's employer base, and the massive UCF alumni network provides reach across the state.

Who Should Apply

Hospitality professionals who want business leadership credentials. Central Florida residents seeking affordable career advancement. Simulation and defense industry professionals. Value-conscious students who want a Florida MBA without the premium price tag.

What to Watch Out For

The UCF brand is strong in Florida but less recognized nationally. Orlando's average salaries lag behind Miami, Tampa, and especially major metros like NYC or SF. The $78K average is modest by MBA standards, though Florida's no-income-tax and low cost of living help. If you're targeting careers outside hospitality, tech, or defense in Central Florida, the program's pipelines are thinner. The small class size limits on-campus networking breadth.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the acceptance rate at UCF MBA?

UCF admits approximately 55% of MBA applicants for the class of 2026. The program enrolls about 33 students in its full-time MBA.

Is UCF good for hospitality careers?

UCF sits in Orlando, the hospitality capital of America. Walt Disney World, Universal Studios, and the convention industry create career opportunities that no other MBA location can match for hospitality management. The College of Hospitality Management at UCF is one of the top-ranked programs globally.

What is the average salary after UCF MBA?

UCF MBA graduates earn an average starting salary of approximately $78,000. Hospitality, consulting, and technology roles in Central Florida drive placement. At $13,500/year in-state tuition, the ROI is strong.
