“A 35-person MBA on one of the most beautiful campuses in America. San Diego Knauss pairs real estate and international business specializations with a city that works in both.”

Program Overview

The University of San Diego's Knauss School sits on a hilltop campus overlooking Mission Bay and the Pacific Ocean. The setting is distractingly beautiful, but the academic strengths are real. Real estate coursework connects to San Diego's active property development market. International business programs tap into the city's Pacific Rim trade connections and proximity to Mexico. The 35-student class gets individualized attention in a 20-month format.

Students average 60 months of prior work experience, which creates a classroom dynamic where professional perspectives inform academic discussions. The Catholic (Augustinian) tradition adds a values dimension. At $39K per year, the tuition is moderate for a private California school. San Diego's economy, driven by biotech, defense, real estate, and international trade, provides the employer base. The program's intimacy is its defining feature: with 35 students, everyone knows each other and every professor knows every student.

Culture & Community

USD Knauss' culture blends California lifestyle with academic seriousness. The campus is among the most beautiful in the country. The 35-person class creates a family-like cohort. San Diego's year-round weather, beaches, and outdoor culture add a quality-of-life dimension. The Catholic university heritage is present but not dominant. Students drawn to San Diego's lifestyle and the program's intimacy self-select into a community that values personal connection alongside professional ambition.

Academics & Curriculum

Real estate finance and development courses are the academic differentiator. International business programs include study abroad and cross-border business projects with Mexican companies. Supply chain management, finance, and marketing round out the concentration options. The 20-month format includes a summer internship window. Faculty in the small program are personally invested in student outcomes. Coursework integrates San Diego's industries as case studies and project partners.

Career Outcomes

San Diego-based employers in real estate, international trade, biotech, and finance are the primary recruiters. Real estate development firms, international trade companies, and the city's biotech sector provide career opportunities. Average salary is approximately $78,000. The intimate alumni network in San Diego is loyal and accessible. Career services in a 35-person program provide individual coaching. Mexico's proximity adds cross-border business opportunities that are unique to this geography.

Who Should Apply

Real estate professionals targeting San Diego's development market. International business candidates interested in Pacific Rim and US-Mexico trade. Anyone drawn to an intimate MBA experience in one of America's best weather cities. Professionals with five or more years of experience who want personalized education.

What to Watch Out For

The $78K average salary is low for a California MBA. The USD Knauss brand is local, strongest in San Diego. The 35-person class means a very small alumni network. If you're targeting LA, Bay Area, or East Coast employers, the program's network doesn't extend easily. The $39K tuition is moderate by California standards but high relative to the salary outcomes. And the 80% employment rate trails programs with more developed career services infrastructure.
