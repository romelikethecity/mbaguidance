“Dayton produces $110K average salaries from a program charging $17K per year. Wright-Patterson Air Force Base and the defense contractor ecosystem explain the numbers. If your career touches defense, aerospace, or government contracting, Dayton's pipeline is the draw.”

Program Overview

Wright-Patterson Air Force Base is one of the largest military installations in the country, employing 30,000+ people and driving billions in defense contracting through the Dayton region. The University of Dayton's MBA program is wired into that ecosystem. Defense contractors, aerospace companies, and government agencies recruit from UD, and the $110K average salary reflects the compensation levels in the defense sector.

The 30-student class gets a Marianist Catholic education with flexible delivery (full-time, part-time, online) at $17K per year. The program's value proposition is specific: if your career involves defense, aerospace, or government contracting, Dayton provides industry access and salary outcomes that justify the degree. The Marianist tradition adds a community and values dimension. Dayton's cost of living is very low, which makes $110K feel like a much larger salary.

Culture & Community

Dayton is a smaller city with deep roots in aviation (the Wright Brothers) and defense. The university's Marianist tradition creates a community-oriented culture with an emphasis on service and ethical leadership. The 30-person class is intimate and supportive. Many students are working professionals at Wright-Patterson or defense contractors who attend evening or online classes. The campus is attractive, and the city's affordability means students live comfortably on modest budgets.

Academics & Curriculum

The curriculum covers business fundamentals with flexibility to specialize through electives. Defense and aerospace industry connections provide case studies and project partners. The Marianist emphasis on service and ethics runs through leadership courses. Finance, operations, and management concentrations serve traditional career paths. The flexible delivery format accommodates military and defense schedules. Faculty engage with the defense contractor community for applied research.

Career Outcomes

Defense contractors (Raytheon, L3Harris, Booz Allen Hamilton), Wright-Patterson AFB civilian roles, and regional employers in healthcare and manufacturing drive placement. Average salary is approximately $110,000. The 85% employment rate reflects strong demand in the defense sector. Career services uses the university's deep relationships with military and contractor employers. The Dayton alumni network is concentrated in defense and aerospace circles.

Who Should Apply

Defense industry professionals who want business leadership credentials. Military personnel and veterans transitioning to business roles. Dayton and Cincinnati area professionals. Anyone targeting government contracting and aerospace management careers.

What to Watch Out For

The $110K salary is driven by defense sector compensation. Graduates entering other industries may see lower numbers. Dayton's job market outside defense is limited. The University of Dayton brand is regional; outside the Midwest and defense circles, it's not a differentiator. If your career goals are in tech, finance hubs, or consulting, the program's networks don't extend to those markets. The city is small, and the social scene is limited.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the acceptance rate at University of Dayton MBA?

University of Dayton admits approximately 75% of MBA applicants for the class of 2026. The program enrolls about 30 students across multiple formats.

Why is the average salary at University of Dayton MBA so high?

Wright-Patterson Air Force Base and the defense contractor ecosystem (Raytheon, L3Harris, Booz Allen Hamilton) drive Dayton's MBA salary outcomes. Defense sector compensation is competitive, and the program has deep industry connections.

What is the average salary after University of Dayton MBA?

University of Dayton MBA graduates earn an average starting salary of approximately $110,000. Defense, aerospace, and government contracting roles drive these outcomes. At $17,000/year tuition, the ROI is exceptional.
