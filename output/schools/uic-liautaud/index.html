Program Overview

UIC Liautaud is the most underpriced MBA in a major city. Illinois residents pay $11,660 per year for a program that produces $101,000 average starting salary and 94% employment. The accelerated format can be completed in as few as 11 months, which means the total investment (tuition plus opportunity cost) is a fraction of what any other Chicago MBA requires. AACSB-accredited, top 6% of business schools worldwide, and it costs less than a semester at Booth.

Chicago's business ecosystem provides the employer base. Fortune 500 headquarters, financial services firms, consulting companies, and a growing tech scene are all within recruiting distance. Finance and business analytics are the academic strengths. The 60-student class gets downtown Chicago access from a campus that's minutes from the Loop. 96% of graduates achieved new roles within four months, a placement rate that would be impressive at twice the price.

Culture & Community

UIC Liautaud's culture is diverse, practical, and urban. The student body reflects Chicago's demographics: multicultural, ambitious, and career-focused. The accelerated format creates intensity that bonds classmates quickly. Downtown Chicago provides the social and professional infrastructure. Students who want an MBA without the suburban campus experience and without the six-figure price tag will find UIC Liautaud's culture refreshingly direct.

Academics & Curriculum

Finance and business analytics are the academic pillars. The accelerated format covers 54 credit hours in as few as 11 months for students on the fastest track. Most students complete in 16 months. The AACSB accreditation places Liautaud in the top 6% of business schools globally. The curriculum is practical and career-focused, with coursework designed to build immediately applicable skills. Faculty bring both research credentials and Chicago business connections.

Career Outcomes

Chicago's corporate base drives placement. Financial services (25%), tech (20%), consulting (18%), and marketing (15%) are the top sectors. The $101,000 average salary and 94% employment rate are remarkable for a program at this price point. 96% of graduates achieved new roles within four months. Chicago's deep employer market means UIC Liautaud graduates compete for the same jobs that graduates from far more expensive programs target, with a fraction of the student debt.

Who Should Apply

Illinois residents who want the cheapest path to a Chicago MBA. Career changers who want rapid re-entry to the workforce. Professionals targeting Chicago's corporate job market. Budget-maximizers who care more about outcomes than brand prestige.

What to Watch Out For

The UIC brand doesn't carry the weight of University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, Booth, or Kellogg. Outside Chicago, the name requires explanation. The accelerated format limits networking time, club involvement, and the traditional MBA social experience. If you want a two-year program with summer internship, deep club engagement, and a nationally recognized brand, UIC Liautaud isn't designed for that. And while the outcomes data is strong, the career services infrastructure is smaller than at higher-ranked programs.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the acceptance rate at UIC Liautaud MBA?

UIC Liautaud admits approximately 65% of MBA applicants for the class of 2026. The program enrolls about 60 students in the full-time accelerated cohort.

How fast can I complete UIC Liautaud MBA?

The UIC Liautaud Accelerated MBA can be completed in as few as 11 months, though most students take 16 months. The 54-credit-hour program covers full MBA fundamentals in a compressed timeline.

What is the average salary after UIC Liautaud MBA?

UIC Liautaud MBA graduates earn an average starting salary of approximately $101,000 with 94% employment. At $12K per year in-state tuition, the program offers one of the highest ROIs in MBA education.
