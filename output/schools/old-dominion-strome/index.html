“Old Dominion Strome sits in Hampton Roads, the largest military installation complex in the world. The defense and maritime industries dominate, and ODU's MBA is built to serve the professionals working in them.”

Program Overview

Norfolk, Virginia is home to Naval Station Norfolk (the world's largest naval base), Newport News Shipbuilding, and the Port of Virginia (one of the East Coast's largest container ports). That military and maritime economy defines the region and shapes ODU's MBA program. The Strome College serves active-duty military, veterans, defense contractors, and logistics professionals with a 21-month MBA that's flexible enough to accommodate deployment schedules.

At $13K per year for in-state students, the tuition is affordable. The 30-student class includes military officers, shipyard engineers, and port logistics professionals. The online and hybrid formats accommodate the mobility of military careers. ODU's 85% acceptance rate makes it accessible, and the program values professional military experience alongside academic credentials. Hampton Roads may not be glamorous, but the employer base is enormous and defense-funded.

Culture & Community

ODU's culture is military-aware in ways that civilian programs aren't. Military service is respected and common. The 30-person class includes active-duty officers, veterans, and defense civilians. The Norfolk area is military-friendly: restaurants, services, and communities cater to service members and their families. The MBA culture is practical and disciplined. Students who value mission-driven work and hierarchical leadership will find the environment familiar.

Academics & Curriculum

Defense management coursework addresses acquisition, contracting, and military systems management. Port and maritime logistics courses connect to Hampton Roads' shipping infrastructure. Online and hybrid delivery accommodates military schedules. Core business courses cover fundamentals with a practical emphasis. Faculty bring connections to the defense and maritime industries. The 21-month format provides a focused timeline.

Career Outcomes

Defense contractors (Huntington Ingalls, BAE Systems, Booz Allen Hamilton), the US Navy, and port logistics companies provide the employer pipeline. Average salary is approximately $76,000. The 78% employment rate reflects Hampton Roads' defense-dependent economy. Career services understands military-to-civilian transitions. The ODU alumni network in defense and government circles is concentrated and loyal.

Who Should Apply

Active-duty military seeking business credentials. Veterans transitioning to defense industry civilian roles. Defense contractor professionals in Hampton Roads. Logistics and maritime professionals targeting the Port of Virginia ecosystem.

What to Watch Out For

Hampton Roads' economy is heavily defense-dependent. Sequestration and defense budget cuts directly impact the job market. The ODU brand is regional; outside Virginia and defense circles, it doesn't carry weight. The $76K salary is modest. If your career goals are in tech, finance, or industries without defense exposure, ODU's positioning doesn't apply. The area's job market is cyclical with defense spending.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the acceptance rate at Old Dominion Strome MBA?

Old Dominion Strome admits approximately 85% of MBA applicants for the class of 2026. The program enrolls about 30 students and is one of the most accessible MBAs in Virginia.

Is Old Dominion Strome good for military professionals?

ODU Strome is designed for military and defense professionals. The program's flexible format accommodates deployment schedules, the curriculum includes defense management coursework, and Hampton Roads' military installations provide career pipelines.

What is the average salary after Old Dominion Strome MBA?

Old Dominion Strome MBA graduates earn an average starting salary of approximately $76,000. Defense, military civilian, and logistics roles in Hampton Roads drive placement.
