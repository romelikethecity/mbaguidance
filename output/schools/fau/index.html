Program Overview

Boca Raton sits in Palm Beach County, one of the wealthiest counties in America. Wealth management firms, real estate developers, and financial services companies line the coast from West Palm Beach to Fort Lauderdale. FAU's MBA program connects to that economy with concentrations in real estate, finance, and management at $11K per year for Florida residents.

The 45-student class averages 8+ years of work experience, which makes classroom discussions richer and the peer network more valuable. The experienced student body means you're learning alongside professionals who've built businesses, managed teams, and navigated real careers. FAU's campus in Boca is modern and comfortable. South Florida's no-income-tax environment and year-round weather add lifestyle appeal to the value proposition.

Culture & Community

FAU's culture is professional and experienced. The average student has 8+ years of work experience, which shifts conversations from theory to application. Boca Raton is affluent, suburban, and comfortable. The 45-person class includes real estate professionals, financial advisors, and executives from South Florida companies. The social scene is more country club than college bar. Students who value professional maturity over youthful energy will find the culture rewarding.

Academics & Curriculum

Real estate finance and development courses connect to South Florida's active property market. Finance and management concentrations serve broader career goals. The experienced student body means classroom discussions draw on real industry challenges. Faculty bring both academic credentials and connections to the South Florida business community. The curriculum is practical and career-focused.

Career Outcomes

South Florida's wealth management firms, real estate developers, healthcare companies (Tenet Healthcare, Cleveland Clinic Florida), and financial services drive placement. Average salary is approximately $78,000. The 80% employment rate reflects the regional market. The FAU alumni network in South Florida is extensive (the university has 180,000+ alumni). Career services connects students to the Palm Beach and Broward County employer base.

Who Should Apply

South Florida professionals with 5+ years of experience who want career advancement. Real estate professionals targeting the development and finance side. Finance professionals in Palm Beach County. Florida residents who want maximum value.

What to Watch Out For

The FAU brand is local to South Florida. Outside the region, it doesn't differentiate. The $78K salary is modest, though Florida's no-income-tax environment helps. If you're targeting national firms or careers outside South Florida, FAU's network doesn't travel well. South Florida's cost of living has risen, especially in Palm Beach County. And the older student body means less of the traditional MBA career-switching support.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the acceptance rate at FAU MBA?

FAU admits approximately 69% of MBA applicants for the class of 2026. The program enrolls about 45 students with an average of 8+ years of prior work experience.

Is FAU good for real estate careers?

FAU's Boca Raton location in Palm Beach County provides direct access to South Florida's real estate development and finance sector. The program's real estate coursework connects to one of the most active property markets in the country.

What is the average salary after FAU MBA?

FAU MBA graduates earn an average starting salary of approximately $78,000. Real estate, finance, and healthcare roles in South Florida drive placement. At $11,000/year in-state tuition, the ROI is strong.
