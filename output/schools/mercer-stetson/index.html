“Mercer Stetson offers an intimate 25-person MBA with Atlanta corporate access at affordable private university pricing. The program is designed for working professionals who want business credentials without the scale of a large university.”

Program Overview

Mercer University's Atlanta campus puts MBA students in the metro area with the 17th-largest economy in the world. The Stetson-Hatcher School offers a 25-person class where faculty know every student, in a city where Coca-Cola, Delta, Home Depot, and UPS are all within commuting distance. That combination of intimacy and access is the value proposition.

At $18K per year, Mercer is affordable for a private university. The program offers both on-campus and online formats, serving working professionals across Georgia. The Baptist tradition emphasizes ethics and community. The program is smaller and less known than Emory, Georgia Tech, or Kennesaw State in the Atlanta market, but for students who value personalized attention over brand prestige, Mercer delivers a different experience.

Culture & Community

Mercer's Atlanta campus is in a professional setting near Buckhead and Midtown. The 25-person class creates a family-like cohort where collaboration is the norm. The Baptist heritage shapes a community that values integrity and service. Students tend to be working professionals with Atlanta-area careers who attend evening or online classes. The culture is supportive and practical, focused on career advancement rather than academic competition.

Academics & Curriculum

Core business courses cover fundamentals with flexibility for individual career goals. The small class size allows faculty to tailor discussions to student interests and industries. Online delivery is polished for working professionals. The curriculum is general management focused, without specialized concentrations. Faculty bring both academic and industry credentials. The program is AACSB-accredited, which provides quality assurance at this tier.

Career Outcomes

Atlanta's corporate market provides career opportunities across industries. Average salary is approximately $72,000. The 77% employment rate reflects the competitive Atlanta market and the program's newer profile. Career services is personalized for the small cohort. The Mercer alumni network in Georgia is loyal, particularly among law and healthcare professionals (Mercer's law and medical schools are well-regarded). Atlanta's economic growth benefits all MBA programs in the market.

Who Should Apply

Georgia professionals who want an intimate MBA experience. Working professionals who prefer a 25-person class. Career advancers who value personalized attention over brand prestige. Candidates who want an affordable private university option in Atlanta.

What to Watch Out For

Mercer's MBA brand is eclipsed by Emory, Georgia Tech, and even Kennesaw State in the Atlanta market. The $72K salary is modest, and the 77% employment rate trails competitors. Outside Georgia, the Mercer MBA brand is not widely recognized. If you need the credentialing power of a top-tier MBA or the broad alumni network of a larger program, Mercer can't compete at that level.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the acceptance rate at Mercer Stetson MBA?

Mercer Stetson admits approximately 75% of MBA applicants for the class of 2026. The program enrolls about 25 students.

Is Mercer Stetson good for Atlanta careers?

Mercer's Atlanta campus provides access to the metro area's corporate base, though the program is smaller and less established than Emory or Georgia Tech. For working professionals seeking advancement in Atlanta, the personalized attention and affordable tuition are the draws.

What is the average salary after Mercer Stetson MBA?

Mercer Stetson MBA graduates earn an average starting salary of approximately $72,000. Corporate, consulting, and healthcare roles in Atlanta drive placement.
