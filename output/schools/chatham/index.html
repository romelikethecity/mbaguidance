Program Overview

Chatham University was founded in 1869 as the Pennsylvania Female College. That women's leadership heritage shapes the MBA program's culture, which emphasizes diverse leadership, inclusive management, and gender equity in business. The program is co-ed but the founding tradition attracts students who value those principles. The MBA costs $16,500 per year, making it one of the most affordable private MBAs in the Northeast.

Pittsburgh's corporate base (UPMC, PNC, Duquesne Light, the tech sector) provides the employer backdrop. The 25-student class gets personalized attention on Chatham's Shadyside campus, one of Pittsburgh's most walkable neighborhoods. Online and on-campus formats provide flexibility. The program is small, personal, and values-driven in a city that's affordable and growing.

Culture & Community

Chatham's culture is inclusive, values-driven, and community-oriented. The women's leadership heritage creates an environment where diverse perspectives are expected and respected. The Shadyside campus is in one of Pittsburgh's best neighborhoods: walkable, with shops and restaurants along Walnut Street. The 25-person class bonds through shared values and small-class intimacy. The culture is progressive and supportive. Students who value inclusive leadership and personal attention will find Chatham's environment distinctive.

Academics & Curriculum

Core business courses cover fundamentals with an emphasis on ethical leadership and inclusive management. Online and on-campus delivery provides flexibility. The curriculum is general management focused without deep specialized concentrations. Faculty are accessible and invested in the small cohort's success. The program's heritage in women's education informs leadership development coursework. Practical and straightforward, designed for career advancement.

Career Outcomes

Pittsburgh's healthcare (UPMC), financial services (PNC), energy, and tech sectors provide career options. Average salary is approximately $65,000. The 78% employment rate reflects Pittsburgh's competitive market. Career services is personalized for the small cohort. The Chatham alumni network is concentrated in Pittsburgh and the Northeast. The women's leadership heritage creates connections in organizations that prioritize gender equity and diverse leadership.

Who Should Apply

Leaders who value inclusive management and women's leadership heritage. Pittsburgh professionals seeking affordable private MBA credentials. Working professionals who need flexible online/on-campus options. Anyone drawn to a small, values-driven MBA community.

What to Watch Out For

The Chatham MBA brand is hyperlocal. Outside Pittsburgh, it's not recognized in MBA recruiting. The $65K salary is low, and the ROI requires careful analysis even at $16.5K tuition. If you need a credential that carries weight nationally or opens doors at major firms, Chatham can't compete at that level. The program is a career advancement tool for professionals who already have a career, not a career-launching brand.

What is the acceptance rate at Chatham MBA?

Chatham admits approximately 80% of MBA applicants for the class of 2026. The program enrolls about 25 students. The GMAT is not required for admission.

Is Chatham a women's university?

Chatham was founded as a women's college in 1869. The MBA program is co-ed, but the women's leadership heritage shapes the culture, emphasizing inclusive management, diverse perspectives, and gender equity in business leadership.

Chatham MBA graduates earn an average starting salary of approximately $65,000. Healthcare, financial services, and energy roles in Pittsburgh drive placement.
