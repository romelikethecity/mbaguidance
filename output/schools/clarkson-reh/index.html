Program Overview

Clarkson is an engineering university in northern New York, near the Canadian border. The Reh School's one-year MBA is designed for STEM professionals who want business leadership skills without spending two years in school. The 12-month format eliminates a year of opportunity cost, which matters for engineers and scientists who are already earning good salaries.

The 30-student class gets supply chain and engineering management concentrations that draw on the university's technical strengths. At $38K total (one year), the investment is comparable to many two-year programs' single-year tuition. Potsdam is remote and cold, which is the tradeoff for an intimate, focused MBA experience. The 85% employment rate suggests graduates find roles efficiently despite the location.

Culture & Community

Potsdam is a small town in the North Country, with cold winters and limited urban amenities. The culture is focused and academic. The 30-person class bonds through the intensity of the one-year format. Clarkson's engineering heritage shapes the MBA culture: analytical, practical, and solution-oriented. Students who want urban excitement should look elsewhere. Students who want a focused year of business education in a close-knit environment will find Clarkson's culture fits.

Academics & Curriculum

Engineering management and supply chain courses are the academic strengths. Core business courses cover fundamentals in the compressed one-year timeline. Cross-registration with engineering programs creates technical-business combinations. The accelerated format is intense: no summer break, continuous coursework. Faculty bring connections to manufacturing, supply chain, and technology industries. The curriculum is efficient and practical.

Career Outcomes

Manufacturing companies, supply chain firms, technology companies, and engineering organizations in the Northeast provide the employer pipeline. Average salary is approximately $71,000. The 85% employment rate is strong for this tier and reflects the demand for MBAs with technical backgrounds. Career services uses the Clarkson engineering alumni network. The brand carries weight in engineering and manufacturing circles.

Who Should Apply

Engineers and STEM professionals who want business credentials in one year. Supply chain professionals seeking leadership roles. Accelerated-timeline seekers who want to minimize time out of the workforce. Technical professionals who value Clarkson's engineering reputation.

What to Watch Out For

Potsdam's isolation is significant. The town is small, remote, and cold. The local job market is minimal, and all graduates relocate. At $38K for the full program, it's competitive with two-year schools' per-year costs, but the total investment in a remote location requires commitment. The Clarkson brand is strong in engineering but less recognized as a business school. If you're targeting finance, consulting, or non-technical business roles, the engineering-heavy brand may not help.
