Program Overview

Iowa Tippie produces a statistic that should make expensive MBA programs uncomfortable: $113,000 average starting salary from a program that costs $23,000 a year in-state. That salary-to-tuition ratio is among the best in MBA education. The program has been climbing in rankings, and the outcomes data explains why. Finance, analytics, and marketing are the core strengths, taught by a faculty that's deeply published and actively consulting.

The 65-student class is large enough for diverse perspectives but small enough that faculty know every student by name. Iowa City is a classic college town: affordable, walkable, and built around the university. The 21-month format includes a summer internship window. Flexible format options (full-time, part-time, online) let working professionals access the same faculty and curriculum without relocating.

Culture & Community

Iowa Tippie's culture is Midwestern in the best sense: collaborative, unpretentious, and hardworking. The 65-person class builds tight connections through group projects and social events. Iowa City is one of the most affordable college towns in the country, and the cultural scene (writers' workshops, local restaurants, Big Ten athletics) provides more social life than the geography might suggest. Students who prioritize substance over status will find their people here.

Academics & Curriculum

Analytics courses are the academic headline. Tippie's business analytics program has been gaining national recognition, and the curriculum integrates data science tools into traditional MBA coursework. Finance and marketing round out the core strengths. Faculty accessibility in the 65-person program means you're getting mentorship alongside instruction. The Iowa Electronic Markets, a real-money prediction market run by the business school, is used as a teaching lab for finance and economics courses.

Career Outcomes

Finance (25%), analytics (20%), marketing (18%), and consulting (15%) are the primary placement sectors. The $113,000 average salary reflects strong outcomes in financial services and analytics roles across the Midwest and nationally. Major employers include Principal Financial Group, Collins Aerospace, and regional consulting firms. Tippie's career services have a strong track record of placing graduates into Chicago, Minneapolis, and Des Moines markets.

Who Should Apply

ROI-maximizers who want top-quartile salary outcomes at bottom-quartile tuition. Analytics and finance professionals targeting Midwest careers. Anyone who values academic quality and career outcomes over brand prestige. Working professionals who want flexible format options from a credible program.

What to Watch Out For

Iowa City is small and far from major business hubs. If you're targeting New York, San Francisco, or LA, you'll need to travel for recruiting events. The Tippie brand is strongest in the Midwest. National recognition is growing but not yet at the level of peer programs with stronger marketing. And the affordable college town lifestyle that makes Tippie a great value also means limited local career opportunities after graduation.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the acceptance rate at Iowa Tippie MBA?

Iowa Tippie admits approximately 55% of MBA applicants for the class of 2026. The program enrolls about 65 students in the full-time cohort.

What is the average salary after Iowa Tippie MBA?

Iowa Tippie graduates earn an average starting salary of approximately $113,000, one of the strongest salary outcomes relative to tuition cost in MBA education. Finance, analytics, and marketing roles drive the highest compensation.

Is Iowa Tippie MBA a good value?

At approximately $23,000 per year in-state tuition with $113,000 average starting salary, Iowa Tippie's ROI is among the best in ranked MBA programs. The salary-to-tuition ratio outperforms many programs ranked significantly higher.
