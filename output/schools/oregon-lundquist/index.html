Program Overview

Oregon Lundquist built its identity around sustainability before sustainability became a corporate buzzword. The Center for Sustainable Business Practices is nationally recognized, and the curriculum integrates environmental and social responsibility into core business courses. This attracts a self-selecting cohort: people who want MBA skills and care about how those skills are applied. The result is a program with a distinctive culture that's different from the finance-and-consulting pipeline at most business schools.

Sports business is the other strength. Eugene is the birthplace of Nike, and Oregon's athletic brand creates connections to the sports industry that most MBA programs can't match. The "learning by doing" approach puts students on real projects with real companies from day one. At $18K in-state tuition, the program offers Pacific Northwest value. The 55-student class is small, the faculty is accessible, and Eugene's outdoor lifestyle attracts students who want quality of life alongside career advancement.

Culture & Community

Oregon Lundquist's culture is collaborative, outdoorsy, and values-driven. Students hike, bike, and ski together between classes. Eugene is a small city with a big personality: progressive, affordable, and surrounded by mountains, rivers, and coast. The sustainability focus creates a community that thinks about business differently. If you want an MBA program where conversations about profit include conversations about impact, Lundquist delivers that naturally.

Academics & Curriculum

Sustainable business practices courses are the academic centerpiece. Students choose focus tracks at admission: innovation and entrepreneurship, finance and securities analysis, or advanced strategy and leadership. The sports business program draws on Oregon's connection to Nike, the Pac-12, and the broader athletic industry. Experiential projects with partner companies replace some traditional coursework. Faculty research in sustainability and innovation feeds directly into classroom content.

Career Outcomes

Sustainability-focused companies, sports industry firms, and Pacific Northwest employers are the primary recruiters. Nike, Columbia Sportswear, and Adidas maintain Oregon connections. Tech companies in Portland (90 minutes north) recruit from the program. Consulting (15%), sustainability/CSR (20%), sports/outdoor industry (12%), and tech (18%) are the top sectors. The $85,000 average salary reflects the Pacific Northwest market, where compensation is strong but trails major metro areas.

Who Should Apply

Candidates who want sustainability integrated into their business education from day one. Sports industry professionals or aspiring sports business leaders. Anyone targeting the Pacific Northwest's employer base. Students who want an affordable public MBA with a distinctive culture and values-driven community.

What to Watch Out For

Eugene is a college town, not a business hub. Recruiting for national firms requires travel to Portland, Seattle, or beyond. The Lundquist brand is regional. The 82% employment rate trails programs with stronger career services infrastructure. If your goals are traditional finance or consulting at a major metro firm, Oregon's geographic isolation and sustainability focus may not align. And the sports business opportunity, while real, is niche.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the acceptance rate at Oregon Lundquist MBA?

Oregon Lundquist admits approximately 58% of MBA applicants for the class of 2026. The program enrolls about 55 students.

Is Oregon Lundquist good for sustainability careers?

Oregon Lundquist's Center for Sustainable Business Practices is nationally recognized. Sustainability is integrated into the core curriculum, and the program attracts students and faculty who prioritize responsible business practices. It's one of the strongest MBA options for sustainability-focused careers.

What is the average salary after Oregon Lundquist MBA?

Oregon Lundquist MBA graduates earn an average starting salary of approximately $85,000. Pacific Northwest employers in sustainability, sports, tech, and consulting drive placement outcomes.
