“An MBA in Warren Buffett's hometown. Creighton Heider's Jesuit ethics curriculum sits in a city dominated by Berkshire Hathaway, Mutual of Omaha, and Union Pacific.”

Program Overview

Omaha is headquarters city for Berkshire Hathaway, Mutual of Omaha, Union Pacific, Kiewit Corporation, and ConAgra Brands. That concentration of major companies in a mid-size city creates a job market that's tighter and more accessible than what you'd find in larger metros with more competition. Creighton Heider's MBA serves that market with a Jesuit ethical leadership curriculum that resonates with Omaha's community-oriented business culture.

The 35-student class averages 10 years of work experience, which means classroom discussions draw on seasoned professional perspectives. The Jesuit tradition of ethical leadership is woven throughout the curriculum. Finance, general management, and leadership development are the academic strengths. At $33K total tuition, the program is priced for working professionals who want MBA credentials without the debt burden that larger programs create.

Culture & Community

Omaha's business community is tight-knit and loyalty-driven. Creighton's Jesuit tradition reinforces that community orientation. The 35-person class, with its average 10 years of experience, creates a peer environment that's more mature than most MBA programs. Omaha is affordable, family-friendly, and centered on a business community that values relationships over credentials. The annual Berkshire Hathaway shareholders' meeting is the city's business Super Bowl, and Creighton students and alumni are part of that ecosystem.

Academics & Curriculum

Ethical leadership is the academic thread that runs through every course. The Jesuit approach means moral reasoning and stakeholder analysis appear alongside financial analysis and marketing strategy. Finance courses benefit from Omaha's insurance and financial services concentration. General management and leadership development round out the core. Faculty bring both academic and industry experience, and the small class means direct access to professors throughout the program.

Career Outcomes

Omaha's corporate headquarters provide the career pipeline. Berkshire Hathaway, Mutual of Omaha, Union Pacific, and ConAgra are significant employers. Finance (28%), insurance (15%), management (20%), and operations (12%) are the top sectors. Average salary is approximately $80,000, which goes further in Omaha's low cost of living than it would in most MBA cities. The Creighton alumni network in Nebraska's business community is established and active.

Who Should Apply

Finance professionals targeting Omaha's insurance and financial services sector. Experienced professionals who want a Jesuit ethics-focused MBA. Anyone drawn to Omaha's quality of life and corporate headquarters concentration. Candidates who value mature peer cohorts and personalized attention over program size and national brand.

What to Watch Out For

Omaha is a mid-size city in Nebraska. If your career goals are coastal or international, the geographic limitation is real. The Creighton Heider brand is strongest in the Midwest. The $80K average salary reflects Omaha's compensation levels. The 35-person class means a small alumni network from any given year. And while the ethical leadership focus is a genuine strength, some students may find it limiting if they want a more traditional, strategy-and-analytics-heavy MBA curriculum.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the acceptance rate at Creighton Heider MBA?

Creighton Heider admits approximately 72% of MBA applicants for the class of 2026. The program enrolls about 35 students with an average of 10 years of work experience.

What companies are headquartered in Omaha?

Omaha is home to Berkshire Hathaway, Mutual of Omaha, Union Pacific, Kiewit Corporation, and ConAgra Brands. This concentration of Fortune 500 companies in a mid-size city creates a strong and accessible job market for Creighton Heider MBA graduates.

What is the average salary after Creighton Heider MBA?

Creighton Heider MBA graduates earn an average starting salary of approximately $80,000. Finance, insurance, and management roles at Omaha-headquartered companies drive placement. The low cost of living makes the salary go further than in larger metro areas.
