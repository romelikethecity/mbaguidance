Program Overview

Eight thousand dollars per year. That's the in-state tuition at Kansas State's MBA program. It's not a typo, and it's not a promotional rate. It's the annual cost of an AACSB-accredited MBA at a respected research university. The $78K average salary means you break even on your investment in less than a year. No other MBA program in the country matches this ROI.

Manhattan, Kansas is a college town in the Flint Hills. It's not a business hub, and that's the tradeoff. But Kansas State's agribusiness expertise is nationally recognized. The state's agricultural economy, Cargill's presence, and the university's veterinary and agricultural research create a pipeline for agribusiness careers that no coastal MBA program can replicate. The 53-student class gets solid business fundamentals at a price that eliminates the financial risk of attending business school.

Culture & Community

Manhattan, Kansas is known as "The Little Apple." K-State's culture is welcoming, purple-clad (Wildcats), and grounded. The 53-student class includes a mix of Kansas natives, military-connected students (Fort Riley is nearby), and international students drawn by the value. The town is affordable, safe, and community-oriented. Football Saturdays at Bill Snyder Family Stadium are the social highlight. Students who want urban sophistication won't find it here. Students who want a no-debt MBA will.

Academics & Curriculum

Agribusiness courses draw on Kansas State's agricultural research strengths and the state's farming economy. The online professional MBA is nationally ranked. Core business courses cover fundamentals with a practical emphasis. Finance and management concentrations are available. Faculty bring connections to the agricultural and manufacturing sectors. The curriculum is solid and straightforward, designed to produce competent business leaders at minimal cost.

Career Outcomes

Agribusiness companies (Cargill, ADM), military-connected employers (Fort Riley), manufacturing, and regional healthcare provide the career pipeline. Average salary is approximately $78,000. The 82% employment rate reflects Midwestern employer demand. Career services connects students to the regional network. The K-State alumni network is loyal and concentrated in Kansas, Missouri, and the broader Midwest. For agribusiness roles, the network extends nationally.

Who Should Apply

Value-conscious students who want the best ROI in MBA education. Agribusiness professionals who want business credentials. Military personnel and veterans near Fort Riley. Midwest professionals who plan to build careers in the region.

What to Watch Out For

Manhattan, Kansas is remote. The local job market is tiny, and most graduates relocate. The K-State MBA brand is regional; outside the Midwest and agribusiness circles, it's not a differentiator. If you're targeting tech, consulting, or finance careers in major metros, the program's network doesn't extend there. Out-of-state tuition ($16K) is still excellent, but the geographic isolation is a real consideration.

What is the average salary after Kansas State MBA?

Kansas State MBA graduates earn an average starting salary of approximately $78,000. Agribusiness, manufacturing, and military-connected roles drive placement. At $8,000/year in-state tuition, the ROI is exceptional.
