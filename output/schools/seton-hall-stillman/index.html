“Seton Hall Stillman sits along New Jersey's corporate corridor with a distinctive sports management concentration. The Catholic tradition and proximity to NYC create a program that's practical, values-driven, and connected to the metro's employer base.”

Program Overview

South Orange, New Jersey is a train ride from Penn Station and a short drive from the NJ Turnpike corporate corridor. Seton Hall Stillman's MBA exploits both locations: NYC's employer market and New Jersey's concentration of pharmaceutical, financial services, and consumer goods companies. The sports management concentration is a distinctive draw, connecting to the Meadowlands, Newark, and the metro area's professional sports ecosystem.

The 40-student class gets a Catholic university education where ethics and purpose-driven leadership are woven into the curriculum. At $27K per year, the tuition is moderate for the NYC metro. The program's flexible format serves working professionals in New Jersey and NYC. Seton Hall's basketball tradition creates brand recognition and alumni energy that extends beyond the business school.

Culture & Community

Seton Hall's campus is in the tree-lined suburb of South Orange, 30 minutes from Manhattan. The Catholic tradition creates a community that values service, ethics, and personal development alongside career advancement. The 40-person class includes working professionals from NJ's pharmaceutical companies, financial firms, and sports organizations. The basketball program creates campus energy and alumni loyalty. The culture is professional, community-oriented, and connected to the metro area's diverse business ecosystem.

Academics & Curriculum

Sports management courses are the academic differentiator, covering sports marketing, franchise finance, and event management. Ethics coursework reflects the Catholic tradition. Finance, marketing, and pharmaceutical management concentrations serve the NJ corporate base. The curriculum balances core business skills with specialized concentrations. Faculty connect to New Jersey's corporate community and the NYC metro business ecosystem.

Career Outcomes

NJ's pharmaceutical companies (Johnson & Johnson, Merck, Novartis), financial services firms, and sports organizations provide the employer pipeline. NYC employer access expands the options. Average salary is approximately $78,000. The 82% employment rate reflects metro area competitiveness. Career services draws on the NJ corporate corridor and Seton Hall's loyal alumni network. Sports management graduates find roles in professional teams, leagues, and sports marketing agencies.

Who Should Apply

NJ professionals targeting local career advancement. Sports management enthusiasts who want business credentials in the metro area. Catholics who value faith-integrated business education. Working professionals who need NYC-area access from a suburban campus.

What to Watch Out For

The Seton Hall brand competes with Rutgers, Stevens, and multiple NYC programs for employer attention. The $78K salary is moderate for the metro area's high cost of living. Sports management is a niche that's competitive and often lower-paying than mainstream business roles. Outside the NYC metro, the brand is less recognized. If you're targeting West Coast, tech, or industries without a NJ presence, the network doesn't extend well.
