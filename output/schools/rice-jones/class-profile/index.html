Rice Jones's class of 130 students represents a small, intimate community. The smaller class creates tight-knit relationships and a community where you'll know most of your classmates by name.

Academic Profile

The average GMAT score is 705, with the middle 80% typically ranging from 675 to 725. The average GPA is 3.4. These are averages, meaning roughly half the admitted class scored below these numbers. Strong work experience, compelling essays, and demonstrated leadership can offset below-average test scores.

Most admitted students have 3-7 years of work experience, with an average of approximately 5 years. The admissions committee looks for candidates who've had enough professional experience to contribute meaningfully to classroom discussions but are early enough in their careers to benefit from the MBA's career-changing potential.

Professional Background

Rice Jones attracts students from diverse professional backgrounds. Common pre-MBA industries include consulting, financial services, technology, and non-profit/government. The school particularly values candidates whose backgrounds align with its strengths in Energy, Healthcare, Entrepreneurship, Small Class.

Diversity of background is a deliberate admissions strategy. Schools build classes where a former investment banker sits next to a Peace Corps volunteer sits next to a software engineer. This diversity is the source of the MBA classroom's value.

What This Means for Applicants

Understanding the class profile helps you position your application. If your GMAT is below 705, consider retaking or emphasizing other strengths. If you come from an over-represented background (consulting, banking), you'll need to differentiate on leadership, personal story, and school fit. If you come from an under-represented background (military, non-profit, healthcare), that diversity is an asset.

The class profile also tells you what your learning environment will look like. A class of 130 with these academic credentials means strong analytical foundations with diverse perspectives.
