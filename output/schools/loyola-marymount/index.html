Program Overview

Loyola Marymount's overall MBA ranking doesn't tell the full story. The US News specialty rankings paint a different picture: #10 in International Business, #11 in Entrepreneurship, #16 in Management, #18 in Accounting, #20 in Finance, #20 in Marketing, #24 in Business Analytics. Those specialty positions put LMU's academic programs alongside schools ranked 30-50 spots higher in the overall list. The Jesuit tradition and the LA location combine to create a program that outperforms its headline number.

The 29-student class is intimate. 60% women (rare gender balance in MBA education). Los Angeles provides the career backdrop: entertainment, tech, financial services, healthcare, and international trade are all accessible from the Westside campus. At $44K per year, the tuition is a premium for this ranking tier, but the LA location and specialty rankings justify the investment for students targeting those specific career paths.

Culture & Community

LMU's culture is Jesuit, diverse, and LA-infused. The Westside campus offers ocean views and year-round sunshine. The 29-person class bonds through small-cohort dynamics and LA's social opportunities. The 60% female enrollment creates a gender balance that most MBA programs struggle to achieve. Students who want a values-centered education in the most diverse city in America will find LMU's culture distinctive.

Academics & Curriculum

The specialty ranking strength is the academic story. International business, entrepreneurship, management, and marketing programs are all nationally recognized. The Jesuit approach integrates ethics and social responsibility throughout the curriculum. LA's industries provide case studies and guest speakers. Faculty bring academic credentials alongside industry connections in entertainment, tech, and international business.

Career Outcomes

LA's diverse economy provides the employer base. Entertainment (15%), finance (20%), consulting (15%), tech (18%), and international business (10%) are the top sectors. Average salary is approximately $90,000. The LA alumni network, while smaller than USC Marshall or UCLA Anderson, is loyal and growing. International business graduates tap into LA's Pacific Rim trade connections. Entrepreneurship program graduates access LA's startup ecosystem.

Who Should Apply

Candidates targeting LA careers in entertainment, tech, or international business. International business professionals who want a top-10 ranked program. Entrepreneurs drawn to LA's startup ecosystem. Students who value gender-balanced cohorts and Jesuit values education. Anyone whose career goals align with LMU's specialty strengths rather than overall ranking.

What to Watch Out For

The LMU overall ranking doesn't match the specialty rankings, which creates a perception gap. Outside LA, the LMU business school brand is less recognized than the university's liberal arts reputation. The $44K tuition is high for this ranking tier. LA's cost of living adds to the financial burden. If you're targeting employers who recruit by overall ranking rather than specialty strength, LMU's headline number may limit your access. And the 29-person class, while intimate, means a very small alumni network from any single year.
