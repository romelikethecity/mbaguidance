Loyola Marymount graduates earn an average starting salary of $90,000 with an employment rate of 84% within three months of graduation. These numbers reflect solid outcomes with room for growth as the program matures.

Industry Breakdown

Loyola Marymount's employment report shows graduates entering these industries:

  • Consulting: 25-35%
  • Finance: 15-25%
  • Technology: 15-25%
  • Other: 15-25% (healthcare, corporate, non-profit, entrepreneurship)

Loyola Marymount is particularly strong for careers in LA Careers, International Business, Entrepreneurship. The school's full profile details specific employer relationships and career pipelines.

Salary by Industry

Average starting salaries vary significantly by industry:

  • Consulting: $175,000-$190,000 base + $25,000-$35,000 signing bonus
  • Finance (banking): $175,000 base + $50,000-$65,000 signing bonus
  • Technology: $150,000-$180,000 base + equity/RSUs
  • Corporate (Fortune 500): $130,000-$155,000 base + $15,000-$25,000 signing bonus

The $90,000 average at Loyola Marymount is a blend across all industries. Students entering consulting and finance typically exceed this average, while those entering non-profit or government roles may earn below it. Loyola Marymount Employment: Quick Answers

What's the average salary for Loyola Marymount MBA graduates?

Loyola Marymount graduates earn a median base salary of $90,000, with signing bonuses typically adding $25,000-$35,000. Total first-year compensation lands in the $115K-$135K range depending on industry and role.

What's the employment rate at Loyola Marymount?

84% of Loyola Marymount graduates were employed within three months of graduation. The remainder are typically founders, candidates pursuing additional credentials, or those still actively recruiting.

What industries do Loyola Marymount grads enter?

Loyola Marymount's strongest placement industries are International Business (#10), Entrepreneurship (#11), Management. Industry mix varies by class, with consulting and finance generally leading and technology growing year over year.

What percentage of Loyola Marymount grads go into consulting?

Consulting is among Loyola Marymount's top employment outcomes, with placement rates at top programs ranging from 25% to 35% depending on the year. McKinsey, BCG, and Bain are perennial top employers, joined by Big Four strategy practices and boutique firms.
