Program Overview

The Dallas-Fort Worth metro is home to 24 Fortune 500 companies, an international airport that's a global logistics hub, and a business-friendly tax environment that keeps attracting corporate relocations. UNT Ryan's MBA sits in Denton, 40 miles north of downtown Dallas, with access to that corporate density at $17K per year for in-state students.

The 50-student class gets concentrations in logistics, supply chain management, and business analytics. The DFW region's distribution infrastructure (DFW Airport, Amazon warehouses, trucking networks) makes supply chain a natural strength. The online MBA is nationally recognized. Texas's no-income-tax environment means the $73K salary stretches further than in coastal markets. UNT is one of the largest universities in Texas (42,000+ students), which creates a broad alumni network.

Culture & Community

Denton is a college town with a thriving music scene and affordable living. UNT's culture is creative and diverse (the university has a top-ranked jazz program). The MBA students are practical and career-focused, many commuting from the DFW suburbs. The 50-person class is large enough for diversity. Texas friendliness and Denton's artsy character create a comfortable environment. Students who want big-city energy need to drive to Dallas.

Academics & Curriculum

Logistics and supply chain courses build on DFW's distribution infrastructure. Business analytics concentrations teach data-driven decision making. The online MBA provides flexibility for working professionals. Core courses cover business fundamentals with a practical emphasis. Faculty connect to the DFW corporate community through research and consulting relationships. The AACSB-accredited program is solid at the price point.

Career Outcomes

DFW's corporate headquarters (AT&T, ExxonMobil, American Airlines, Toyota NA) and the region's logistics and distribution companies provide the employer base. Average salary is approximately $73,000. Texas's no-income-tax environment makes the after-tax figure more competitive. The 80% employment rate reflects DFW's strong job market. Career services connects students to the metro area's employer network. The UNT alumni base in Texas is extensive.

Who Should Apply

Texas residents targeting DFW career advancement. Supply chain and logistics professionals in the region. Budget-conscious students who want Texas public university value. Working professionals who need online MBA flexibility.

What to Watch Out For

The UNT brand competes with UT Austin, SMU, TCU, and several other Texas MBA programs for employer attention. Outside Texas, the name doesn't carry weight. The $73K salary is modest by MBA standards, though Texas's cost of living and tax advantages improve the real value. Denton's distance from downtown Dallas limits daily employer access. If you're targeting industries concentrated outside DFW, the program's network doesn't extend well.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the acceptance rate at UNT Ryan MBA?

UNT Ryan admits approximately 65% of MBA applicants for the class of 2026. The program enrolls about 50 students.

Is UNT Ryan good for supply chain careers?

UNT Ryan's logistics and supply chain programs connect to DFW's massive distribution infrastructure. The region's airport, warehouse networks, and corporate headquarters create career pipelines in supply chain management and operations.

What is the average salary after UNT Ryan MBA?

UNT Ryan MBA graduates earn an average starting salary of approximately $73,000. Corporate, supply chain, and consulting roles in the DFW metro drive placement. Texas's no-income-tax environment improves take-home pay.
