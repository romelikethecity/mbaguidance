“Hofstra Zarb sits on Long Island, 25 miles from Manhattan, producing $110K average salaries from a flexible MBA. For Long Island professionals who want NYC-adjacent career outcomes without the NYC tuition, the math works.”

Program Overview

Hempstead, Long Island is 25 miles from Midtown Manhattan. That proximity to the country's largest business market defines Hofstra Zarb's MBA value proposition. Graduates enter the NYC metro job market, where salaries reflect the cost of doing business in America's financial capital. The $110K average starting salary is driven by the geography, not the ranking.

The 35-student class gets a flexible MBA with strong accounting and finance programs. Hofstra's accounting reputation is particularly strong: CPA exam pass rates are competitive with top accounting programs nationally. At $30K per year, the tuition is moderate for the NYC metro. Long Island offers suburban quality of life with LIRR access to Manhattan. The program serves working professionals with evening and weekend options alongside traditional full-time enrollment.

Culture & Community

Hofstra's campus is suburban and leafy, a contrast to Manhattan's intensity. The MBA culture is professional and flexible, accommodating working students who commute from across Long Island and Queens. The 35-person class includes a mix of career changers and working professionals. The university's proximity to NYC creates access to cultural events, networking, and recruiting activities in the city. Long Island's communities provide affordable housing relative to Manhattan.

Academics & Curriculum

Accounting is the academic strength, with CPA preparation integrated into the curriculum. Finance and marketing concentrations serve traditional business career paths. The flexible format allows students to pace their studies over one to three years. Faculty bring connections to the NYC business community. The curriculum is practical and career-focused, designed to produce graduates who can compete in the metro area's demanding job market.

Career Outcomes

NYC metro employers in financial services, accounting firms, healthcare, and consulting drive placement. Big Four accounting firms recruit from Hofstra's accounting program. Average salary is approximately $110,000. The 83% employment rate reflects the competitive NYC job market. Long Island's own corporate base (Northwell Health, Henry Schein, CA Technologies) provides additional options. Career services uses the NYC proximity for employer engagement.

Who Should Apply

Long Island professionals who want to advance without relocating to Manhattan. Accounting professionals targeting CPA credentials with an MBA. NYC metro workers who prefer a suburban campus. Anyone who wants NYC salary outcomes at non-NYC tuition prices.

What to Watch Out For

The Hofstra brand competes with NYU Stern, Columbia, and a dozen other NYC-area MBA programs. Outside the metro, it's not a differentiator. Long Island's commute to Manhattan (60-90 minutes) limits daily employer engagement compared to schools in the city. The $30K tuition is reasonable but not cheap, and the program's value depends on staying in the NYC metro. If you relocate to a different market, the salary premium and network value diminish.
