  • Consulting: 25-35%

Hofstra (Zarb) is particularly strong for careers in NYC Metro Careers, Accounting, Long Island Professionals. The school's full profile details specific employer relationships and career pipelines.

Salary by Industry

Average starting salaries vary significantly by industry:

  • Consulting: $175,000-$190,000 base + $25,000-$35,000 signing bonus
  • Finance (banking): $175,000 base + $50,000-$65,000 signing bonus
  • Technology: $150,000-$180,000 base + equity/RSUs
  • Corporate (Fortune 500): $130,000-$155,000 base + $15,000-$25,000 signing bonus

The $110,000 average at Hofstra (Zarb) is a blend across all industries. Students entering consulting and finance typically exceed this average, while those entering non-profit or government roles may earn below it. Hofstra (Zarb) Employment: Quick Answers

What's the average salary for Hofstra (Zarb) MBA graduates?

Hofstra (Zarb) graduates earn a median base salary of $110,000, with signing bonuses typically adding $25,000-$35,000. Total first-year compensation lands in the $135K-$155K range depending on industry and role.

What's the employment rate at Hofstra (Zarb)?

83% of Hofstra (Zarb) graduates were employed within three months of graduation. The remainder are typically founders, candidates pursuing additional credentials, or those still actively recruiting.

What industries do Hofstra (Zarb) grads enter?

Hofstra (Zarb)'s strongest placement industries are NYC Metro Access, Accounting, Flexible MBA. Industry mix varies by class, with consulting and finance generally leading and technology growing year over year.

What percentage of Hofstra (Zarb) grads go into consulting?

Consulting is among Hofstra (Zarb)'s top employment outcomes, with placement rates at top programs ranging from 25% to 35% depending on the year. McKinsey, BCG, and Bain are perennial top employers, joined by Big Four strategy practices and boutique firms.
