Top 25 MBA Admissions & Career Voices of 2026

The consultants, editors, and educators with the most influence on how candidates evaluate MBA programs and build their applications

Last updated: 2026-04-14 · 25 voices ranked

How We Ranked These Voices

Ranked by direct influence on MBA admissions decisions, cross-list appearances on industry publications (Poets&Quants, TopMBA, US News, Financial Times, Beat the GMAT), verified client outcomes, content reach, and depth of expertise. We evaluated 40+ candidates and filtered for people whose advice actively changes how applicants approach the MBA process.

We evaluated candidates across five dimensions:

  • Topic relevance (required): Must actively contribute to MBA admissions consulting, test prep, or business school journalism.
  • Client outcomes (30%): Verified admissions results and client satisfaction scores from Poets&Quants and other review platforms.
  • Content reach (25%): Published work, podcast audiences, and newsletter subscribers.
  • Community impact (25%): Free resources, public content, and accessibility of advice.
  • Originality (20%): Unique perspectives, insider experience, and differentiated methodologies.

This list is updated annually. Subscribe to MBA Guidance to get notified when we refresh the rankings.

Top 10 Leaders

The most recognized and influential voices in MBA admissions and career strategy.

#1

John Byrne

Founder & Editor-in-Chief at Poets&Quants

MediaRankingsMBA Journalism

Founded Poets&Quants in 2010 and turned it into the most-read publication in MBA admissions. Former editor-in-chief at BusinessWeek and Fast Company. His annual rankings of admissions consultants, MBA programs, and career outcomes have become the reference standard for anyone evaluating business school. If you're applying to an MBA, you've read his work whether you know it or not.

#2

Jeremy Shinewald

Founder & President at mbaMission

Admissions ConsultingEssay StrategyFree Resources

Founded mbaMission in 1999 and built it into the most favorably reviewed large MBA admissions consulting firm for 5 consecutive years on Poets&Quants. 214 positive verified client reviews with a 9.86 satisfaction score. His firm produces more free content (school guides, essay analyses, webinars) than any competitor, making high-quality admissions advice accessible beyond the paying client base.

#3

Sandy Kreisberg

Founder at HBSGuru

HBS ExpertPioneerPoets&Quants

The original MBA admissions consultant. Former Director of Communications at MIT Sloan who started HBSGuru.com in 1995, making him one of the first people to offer MBA admissions consulting as a service. Has placed hundreds of applicants into HBS, Wharton, Stanford, and other M7 schools. His 'Fridays with Sandy' column on Poets&Quants is a long-running fixture in the admissions world.

#4

Founder at Stacy Blackman Consulting

Admissions ConsultingHBSStanford

Kellogg MBA who founded SBC in 2001 and grew it into one of the largest admissions consulting firms in the industry. Her panel of 30+ consultants includes former admissions officers from HBS, Stanford GSB, and Wharton. She's been featured in the Wall Street Journal, Fortune, and Bloomberg Businessweek. The firm's track record spans thousands of successful admits across all top programs.

#5

Founder & CEO at Accepted.com

Admissions PioneerPodcastAccepted.com

Her Admissions Straight Talk podcast has run for years, providing free admissions advice to a broad audience. She was one of the dominant voices on early business school message boards alongside Sandy Kreisberg, helping shape the online MBA admissions advice industry before social media existed.

HBS GradM7 FocusDirect Approach
