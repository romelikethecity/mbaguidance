Why People Compare These Two

Both are Connecticut private university MBAs. Quinnipiac ($26K/year) in Hamden focuses on healthcare administration. Fairfield ($31K/year) in Fairfield County connects to the financial services corridor. Both serve working professionals in the state. The choice is career direction: healthcare vs finance.

The Honest Take

Quinnipiac wins on tuition ($26K vs $31K) and identical salary outcomes ($85K each). Fairfield wins for financial services careers, with proximity to the hedge fund and wealth management corridor. For healthcare administration, Quinnipiac is the stronger choice. For Connecticut finance, Fairfield's location is the advantage. Both produce comparable outcomes at moderate pricing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is better for healthcare?

Quinnipiac's healthcare administration specialization is stronger. Connecticut's insurance corridor (CVS Health/Aetna, Cigna) creates healthcare career pipelines from Quinnipiac's program.

Which is better for finance?

Fairfield's location in the financial services corridor (Greenwich to Stamford hedge funds, wealth management) gives it the edge for finance careers.

Which is more affordable?

Quinnipiac ($26K/year) is slightly cheaper than Fairfield ($31K/year). Salary outcomes are comparable ($85K each).
