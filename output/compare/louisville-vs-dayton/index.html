Why People Compare These Two

Both are Midwestern MBAs with accelerated options. Louisville's 13-month format includes a paid internship. Dayton produces $110K salaries connected to the defense sector. Louisville charges $19K flat-rate (any state); Dayton charges $17K. The career direction (general business vs defense) and salary expectations should drive the decision.

The Honest Take

Dayton wins on salary ($110K vs $72K), but those outcomes are defense-sector driven. If you're targeting defense and aerospace, Dayton is the obvious choice. Louisville's 13-month format with paid internship is more efficient for career changers targeting general business roles. Louisville's flat-rate tuition is a unique advantage for out-of-state students.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which has higher salaries?

Dayton ($110K) significantly outearns Louisville ($72K). The gap is driven by defense sector compensation at Wright-Patterson AFB.

Which is faster?

Louisville's 13-month format is one of the fastest MBA programs in the country. Dayton also offers an accelerated option but standard format takes longer.

Which is better for non-defense careers?

Louisville is better for general business career changers. Dayton's outcomes are concentrated in the defense sector. Outside defense, Louisville provides broader career optionality.
