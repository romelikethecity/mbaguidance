Why People Compare These Two

LMU (#96) and Pepperdine Graziadio (#72) are both faith-based MBA programs in LA's Westside. Pepperdine in Malibu and LMU in Westchester serve candidates who want values-driven business education in Southern California. Pepperdine outranks LMU by 24 spots, but both serve LA's local market.

Culture and Values

LMU's Jesuit tradition emphasizes social justice and ethical leadership alongside business fundamentals. Pepperdine's Church of Christ heritage shapes a culture that values faith, service, and entrepreneurship. Both are genuine about their missions. The faith dimension is a real part of the experience at both schools, which attracts some candidates and may not appeal to others.

The Honest Take

Pepperdine's higher ranking and stronger entrepreneurship program give it the edge for most career paths. LMU is worth considering if you prefer the Jesuit tradition, want to be in LA's Westside urban setting (vs Malibu's isolation), or receive better financial aid. For LA careers, Pepperdine's broader alumni network provides more connections.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which ranks higher?

Pepperdine Graziadio (#72) outranks LMU (#96). The 24-spot gap is meaningful. Pepperdine has broader brand recognition nationally.

Which is better for entrepreneurship?

Pepperdine has stronger entrepreneurship curriculum and resources. LMU is developing its entrepreneurship offerings but Pepperdine has more history in this area.

Which campus is better?

LMU's Westchester campus is in urban LA with easy access to the city. Pepperdine's Malibu campus is beautiful but isolated. Choose based on whether you prefer urban access or scenic seclusion.
