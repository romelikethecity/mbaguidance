Why People Compare These Two

Both schools are in Washington, DC, and both benefit from the city's unique employer ecosystem. The comparison hinges on Georgetown's significantly higher ranking (#20 vs #48) and whether that premium justifies the cost difference. For DC-focused careers, both schools provide local access.

Brand and Recruiting

Georgetown's brand is globally recognized. GW's brand is regionally strong. For government consulting, both schools have strong pipelines, but Georgetown's McDonough opens more doors at the major firms (McKinsey, BCG, Deloitte Federal). GW places well into federal agencies, nonprofit organizations, and policy roles that don't require top-20 business school credentials.

The Honest Take

Georgetown is the better program by every traditional measure. GW is worth considering if Georgetown isn't in reach or if GW offers significantly better financial aid. For DC government careers where the work matters more than the school name, GW produces strong outcomes at a lower total cost. For private sector careers, Georgetown's brand premium is worth paying for.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the ranking gap too large to compare?

The 28-spot gap (#20 vs #48) is significant. Georgetown is a top-20 national program; GW is a solid regional program. They're both in DC, which creates overlap, but the career outcomes and recruiting access differ meaningfully.

Which is better for government careers?

Georgetown for prestigious government roles (World Bank, IMF, senior policy positions). GW for federal agency careers, nonprofit management, and policy implementation roles. The prestige hierarchy matters less in government than in the private sector.

Which is more affordable?

GW's tuition is lower, and they often offer more generous scholarships to attract candidates away from Georgetown. If cost is a major factor, GW can deliver strong DC-area outcomes at a lower price.
