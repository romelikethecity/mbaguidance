Why People Compare These Two

Fox (#57) and LeBow (#88) are both Philadelphia MBA programs competing for candidates who want to stay in the region. The 31-spot ranking gap favors Temple, but both schools serve Philadelphia's corporate market and benefit from the city's healthcare, financial services, and pharma industries. Drexel's co-op model provides a distinctive experiential component.

Career Outcomes

Temple Fox has a larger alumni network in the Philadelphia area and stronger brand recognition regionally. Drexel LeBow benefits from Drexel University's engineering and health sciences programs, creating cross-disciplinary opportunities. Both place well into Philadelphia-area employers, but Fox's higher ranking opens more doors for competitive roles.

The Honest Take

Fox is the better-ranked, better-known program with stronger career outcomes. LeBow makes sense for candidates who value Drexel's co-op model, who want to combine an MBA with engineering or health sciences, or who receive significantly better financial aid. For Philadelphia careers, Fox is the safer bet.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which ranks higher?

Temple Fox (#57) outranks Drexel LeBow (#88) by a wide margin. The gap matters for competitive recruiting. Both are recognized in Philadelphia.

Is Drexel's co-op valuable for MBA students?

Drexel's co-op model provides paid work experience during the program, which is unique at the MBA level. For career changers who need to demonstrate new skills, a co-op placement can be a real differentiator on your resume.

Which has better pharma connections?

Both benefit from Philadelphia's pharma presence. Temple Fox has the edge due to its larger alumni network and stronger employer relationships. Drexel's health sciences integration provides a different kind of access.
