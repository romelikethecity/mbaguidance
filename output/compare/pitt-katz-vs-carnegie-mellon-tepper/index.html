Why People Compare These Two

Katz (#54) and Tepper (#18) are both in Pittsburgh, but they're in different leagues. The 36-spot ranking gap is the largest on this comparison page. Candidates compare them because Pittsburgh's affordable cost of living makes both programs attractive, and Katz's lower price creates a value argument for budget-conscious applicants.

Academics and Approach

Tepper is one of the most quantitative MBA programs in the country, with deep strength in operations research, data analytics, and technology management. Katz offers a solid general management education with growing strength in healthcare management and supply chain. The academic experiences are meaningfully different in rigor and specialization.

Career Outcomes

Tepper places 25%+ into tech and has strong consulting and finance pipelines. Katz places well into Pittsburgh-area employers, healthcare organizations, and regional corporations. Tepper recruits nationally; Katz recruits primarily regionally. The salary gap reflects this difference.

The Honest Take

If you can get into Tepper, go to Tepper. The ranking gap translates to meaningfully better career access and earning potential. Katz is a reasonable choice for candidates who want to stay in Pittsburgh, work in healthcare, or who receive significant scholarship money that changes the ROI calculation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the ranking gap too large to compare?

Yes, these programs are in different tiers. Tepper is a top-20 national program; Katz is a regional program. The comparison exists because they share a city, not because they're comparable in outcomes. If admitted to both, Tepper is the clear choice.

Which is better for healthcare?

Katz has strong connections to UPMC, Pittsburgh's dominant healthcare system. Tepper places more graduates into healthcare consulting and health tech. Different parts of the healthcare ecosystem.

How much cheaper is Katz?

Katz's tuition is significantly lower, especially for Pennsylvania residents. With Pittsburgh's low cost of living and Katz's scholarship generosity, the total investment can be half of Tepper's. Whether the savings justify the ranking gap depends on your career goals.
