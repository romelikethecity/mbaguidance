Why People Compare These Two

Howard (#81) and Kogod (#100) are both Washington, DC MBA programs that compete with Georgetown and GW for local candidates. Howard's distinction as the nation's most prestigious HBCU creates a unique alumni network. Kogod at American University serves DC's international community with strength in global business and policy.

Distinctive Value

Howard's MBA carries weight that rankings don't fully capture. Its alumni network in corporate diversity leadership, government, and finance is disproportionately powerful. Companies actively recruit Howard MBA graduates for leadership development programs. Kogod's international business focus and DC policy connections serve a different kind of candidate.

The Honest Take

Howard's brand and network provide career advantages that transcend its numerical ranking, especially for candidates entering corporate leadership programs or government service. Kogod serves candidates focused on international business and DC policy. For DC government careers, both schools provide solid access. The choice depends on which network and culture resonates with you.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is better ranked?

Howard (#81) outranks American Kogod (#100). Both are in the lower tier nationally but well-known in DC. Howard's brand carries more weight than its ranking suggests.

Is Howard's network special?

Yes. Howard's alumni network in corporate America, government, and law is one of the most engaged and loyal of any university. For career advancement, this network provides access that other schools at similar rankings don't offer.

Which is better for international careers?

Kogod at American University has stronger international business curriculum and connections to DC's diplomatic and development community. Howard's international connections are growing but Kogod has deeper institutional ties in this area.
