Why People Compare These Two

Both are affordable Mid-South MBA programs. Ole Miss ($13K/year before assistantships) in Oxford, MS and Memphis ($12.3K/year) in Memphis, TN serve overlapping regional markets. Memphis wins on salary ($92K vs $72K). Ole Miss wins on net cost with Palmer assistantships.

The Honest Take

Memphis produces significantly higher salary outcomes ($92K vs $72K) driven by the FedEx supply chain pipeline. Ole Miss's Palmer assistantships reduce net cost to roughly $3,250/year, making it almost free. For supply chain careers, Memphis is the clear choice. For students who want the lowest possible net cost and strong online MBA delivery, Ole Miss with Palmer funding is the value play.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which has better salary outcomes?

Memphis Fogelman ($92K) significantly outearns Ole Miss ($72K). The gap reflects Memphis's FedEx and supply chain salary premium.

Which is cheaper after aid?

Ole Miss with Palmer assistantships (net ~$3,250/year) is cheaper than Memphis ($12,300/year). Without Palmer funding, they're comparable.

Which has a better online MBA?

Ole Miss's online MBA is ranked #33 nationally by US News. Memphis has a solid online option but doesn't match Ole Miss's online ranking.
