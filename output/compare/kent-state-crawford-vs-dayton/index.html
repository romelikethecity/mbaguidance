Why People Compare These Two

Both are Ohio MBA programs. Kent State ($20K/year) in Northeast Ohio focuses on analytics. Dayton ($17K/year) in Southwest Ohio produces $110K salaries from defense industry connections. The salary gap ($68K vs $110K) is enormous and driven by Dayton's defense sector pipeline.

The Honest Take

If defense and aerospace interest you, Dayton's $110K outcomes make it the clear choice at lower tuition. If you're in Northeast Ohio targeting Cleveland/Akron employers, Kent State's proximity matters more than Dayton's defense premium. The programs serve different Ohio markets and different career paths. Compare them on career direction, not just ranking.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is Dayton's salary so much higher?

Dayton's $110K average is driven by defense sector compensation from Wright-Patterson AFB and contractors like Raytheon and L3Harris. Kent State's $68K reflects Northeast Ohio's non-defense economy.

Which is more affordable?

Dayton ($17K/year) is slightly cheaper than Kent State ($20K/year). Dayton's higher salary makes it the ROI winner by a wide margin.

Which is better for analytics?

Kent State Crawford has dedicated business analytics and digital transformation concentrations. Dayton does not emphasize analytics specifically.
