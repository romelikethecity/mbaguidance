# Content Strategy — MBA Guidance

*Framework based on marketingskills content-strategy skill + Harry Dry landing page formula.*

---

## Site Positioning

**What we are:** The MBA resource Rome Thorndike wishes existed when he was applying.
**Who it's for:** Aspiring MBAs (25-32) researching programs, and recent MBA grads navigating first jobs.
**Why they stay:** Real data, honest verdicts, zero fluff. Every page earns its existence by answering a question admissions pages won't.

---

## Content Pillars (Hub-and-Spoke)

### Pillar 1: School Intelligence
- **Hub:** `/schools/` (all 50 profiles)
- **Spokes:** Individual school profiles, school subpages (curriculum, careers, culture, cost, essays, FAQ)
- **Search pattern:** "[school name] MBA", "[school] acceptance rate", "[school] average salary"

### Pillar 2: Head-to-Head Comparisons
- **Hub:** `/compare/` (all 35 comparisons)
- **Spokes:** Individual comparison pages
- **Search pattern:** "[school A] vs [school B]", "[school] vs [school] MBA"

### Pillar 3: Application Strategy
- **Hub:** `/guides/` (22 guides)
- **Spokes:** GMAT vs GRE, application timeline, essay writing, career-specific guides, etc.
- **Search pattern:** "how to get into [school]", "MBA essay tips", "GMAT vs GRE for MBA"

### Pillar 4: Career Outcomes & ROI
- **Hub:** `/blog/` + `/tools/`
- **Spokes:** "Is [School] Worth It?" posts, ROI calculator, salary guides
- **Search pattern:** "is [school] MBA worth it", "MBA ROI calculator", "MBA salary [year]"

### Pillar 5: Rankings & Data
- **Hub:** `/rankings/overall/`
- **Spokes:** Category rankings (finance, tech, consulting, entrepreneurship, international)
- **Search pattern:** "best MBA programs [year]", "top MBA for [career]"

## Searchable vs Shareable

### Searchable (SEO-first, captures existing demand)
- School profiles → "[school name] MBA"
- Comparison pages → "[school A] vs [school B]"
- Guide pages → "GMAT vs GRE", "MBA application timeline"
- ROI posts → "is [school] worth it [year]"
- Rankings → "best MBA programs [year]"

### Shareable (creates demand, social/newsletter distribution)
- Opinionated verdicts and hot takes
- Salary/ROI data visualizations
- "The honest truth about [school]" content
- Newsletter: "Who's hiring MBAs this week?"

## Harry Dry Landing Page Formula — Applied to MBA Guidance

### Homepage (Above the Fold)
1. **Title:** Lead with value + address objection (hook formula)
2. **Subtitle:** Explain how we deliver that value
3. **Visual:** Stats ticker with real data (not abstract graphics)
4. **Social Proof:** Author credential (Berkeley Haas MBA), content count (50 schools, 35 comparisons)
5. **CTA:** Pathway cards (Find School, Compare, Career Paths)

### Homepage (Below the Fold)
6. **Features:** Article list, top 10 table, tools
7. **Social Proof:** Data-driven content previews
8. **FAQ:** (on dedicated FAQ pages, linked from homepage)
9. **2nd CTA:** Newsletter signup
10. **Founder's Note:** Author bio section

### The Ultimate Test
> "Would this help me sell if I met the customer in person? If not, remove it."

Every section on the homepage must pass this test. Abstract graphics, vague copy, and filler sections fail it.

## Content Quality Standards

### Every Page Must Have
- Primary keyword in title, H1, and first 100 words
- Meta description 120-160 chars with benefit
- 3-5 internal links minimum
- FAQ section with 3+ questions (where appropriate)
- Author byline
- BreadcrumbList schema
- No AI writing tells (see ROME_WRITING_STYLE.md)

### Minimum Word Counts
| Page Type | Minimum |
|-----------|---------|
| School profile | 1,200 |
| Comparison page | 2,000 |
| Guide page | 1,500 |
| Blog post | 1,500 |
| Ranking page | 800 |
| Hub/index page | 500 |
