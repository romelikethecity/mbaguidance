The San Diego MBA Job Market

San Diego's economy centers on biotech (Illumina, Dexcom, genomics startups), defense (Navy, General Atomics, SPAWAR), and a growing tech scene. Qualcomm is headquartered here. The city's biotech corridor is the second-largest in the US after Boston/Cambridge. Tourism and hospitality add diversity but aren't typical MBA paths.

Cost of Living

Expensive for a non-major metro. Rent runs $2,000-$2,800/month. Cheaper than LA and SF, but still above the national average. The weather (year-round sunshine) is the quality-of-life argument.

Why Get an MBA in San Diego

If biotech or defense is your target industry, San Diego provides direct access. UCSD Rady (#64) draws on the university's world-class biotech research. SDSU Fowler (#93) and USD Knauss (#99) serve the local market at lower price points. Year-round perfect weather and a relaxed West Coast lifestyle are the bonus.

What to Watch Out For

San Diego's corporate market is smaller than LA or SF. For careers outside biotech and defense, you may need to recruit nationally. Public transit is limited. The city's MBA programs are regional in reputation, so manage expectations for national recruiting.

Frequently Asked Questions

What MBA programs are in San Diego?

UCSD Rady (#64), SDSU Fowler (#93), and USD Knauss (#99). Rady is the highest-ranked, with strong biotech connections through UCSD's research ecosystem. Fowler and Knauss serve the local market.

Is San Diego good for biotech MBA careers?

Excellent. San Diego's biotech corridor (Illumina, Dexcom, dozens of genomics startups) is second only to Boston/Cambridge. UCSD Rady graduates have direct access to this ecosystem.

How expensive is San Diego?

Cheaper than LA and SF, but still above the national average. Budget $2,000-$2,800/month for rent. UCSD and SDSU offer in-state tuition for California residents, which provides strong value despite the higher cost of living.
