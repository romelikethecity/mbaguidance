The Central Indiana MBA Job Market

Indiana's economy centers on manufacturing (Eli Lilly, Cummins), healthcare (IU Health, Anthem), and a growing tech scene in Indianapolis. Salesforce has a major Indianapolis presence. The state's three MBA programs produce graduates for Midwest corporate roles, consulting, and operations positions nationally.

Cost of Living

Among the most affordable MBA destinations in the US. Bloomington rent runs $800-$1,200/month. South Bend (near Notre Dame) and West Lafayette (Purdue) are similarly affordable. Indianapolis is slightly higher at $1,000-$1,500. Your MBA dollars stretch further here than almost anywhere.

Why Get an MBA in Central Indiana

Three strong programs in one state. Kelley (#24) has elite career services and the top operations program in the Midwest. Mendoza (#29) offers the Notre Dame brand and alumni network. Krannert (#35) provides STEM-focused education at a public university price. All three offer exceptional value with Indiana's low cost of living.

What to Watch Out For

Bloomington, South Bend, and West Lafayette are all college towns. If you need urban energy, Indianapolis is 1-2 hours from each campus. Most graduates recruit nationally (Chicago, NYC, the coasts) rather than staying in Indiana. Winters are cold and gray.

Frequently Asked Questions

What MBA programs are in Indiana?

Indiana Kelley (#24) in Bloomington, Notre Dame Mendoza (#29) in South Bend, and Purdue Krannert (#35) in West Lafayette. Three top-35 programs in one state is unusual and creates genuine options for Midwest-oriented candidates.

Which Indiana MBA is best?

Kelley (#24) is the highest-ranked, with elite career services and the strongest operations program. Mendoza (#29) offers the Notre Dame brand. Krannert (#35) provides STEM-focused education. The right choice depends on your career goals and cultural preferences.

Is Indiana affordable for an MBA?

Extremely. All three programs offer in-state tuition options (Kelley and Purdue directly, Mendoza through scholarship competitiveness). Cost of living in all three college towns is among the lowest of any MBA destination in the US.
