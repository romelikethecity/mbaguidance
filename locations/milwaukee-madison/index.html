The Milwaukee-Madison MBA Job Market

Wisconsin's economy runs on manufacturing (Harley-Davidson, Rockwell Collins, Johnson Controls), healthcare (Froedtert, Aurora), and finance (Northwestern Mutual, Robert W. Baird). Milwaukee is the corporate center; Madison is the university and government hub. Both cities offer a high quality of life with low costs.

Cost of Living

Very affordable. Madison rent runs $1,100-$1,700/month. Milwaukee is slightly cheaper. Both cities are 30-40% below coastal living costs. For MBA students, this translates to lower debt and more financial flexibility after graduation.

Why Get an MBA in Milwaukee-Madison

Wisconsin (#30) in Madison is a top-30 MBA with nationally ranked supply chain and operations programs. Marquette (#95) in Milwaukee serves the local market with a Jesuit educational tradition. For Midwest manufacturing, supply chain, or healthcare careers, these programs provide direct employer access at affordable prices.

What to Watch Out For

Wisconsin winters are long and cold. The Milwaukee-Madison job market is solid but limited compared to Chicago (90 minutes south). Many Wisconsin MBA graduates end up in Chicago for post-MBA careers, which is easy geographically but means you're competing with Booth, Kellogg, and other Chicago-area programs.

Frequently Asked Questions

What MBA programs are in Wisconsin?

University of Wisconsin (#30) in Madison and Marquette (#95) in Milwaukee. Wisconsin is the significantly higher-ranked program with national supply chain recognition. Marquette serves the Milwaukee business community with a values-driven approach.

Is Madison a good college town for an MBA?

One of the best. Madison consistently ranks among the top college towns in America for quality of life. The campus, lakes, food scene, and outdoor access create an exceptional two-year experience. Milwaukee's urban amenities are 80 minutes away.

Are Wisconsin MBA graduates competitive for Chicago jobs?

Yes. Madison is 2.5 hours from Chicago by car or bus. Wisconsin MBA graduates regularly recruit into Chicago consulting, finance, and corporate roles. The geographic proximity makes Chicago the primary post-MBA market for many Wisconsin graduates.
