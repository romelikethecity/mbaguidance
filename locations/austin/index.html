The Austin MBA Job Market

Austin has transformed from a college town into one of the hottest tech markets in the country. Tesla moved its HQ here. Apple, Google, Amazon, Oracle, and Meta all have major campuses. Dell and Samsung are longtime Austin anchors. The tech job density rivals Seattle and is growing faster than any other market.

Cost of Living

Rising but still cheaper than SF, NYC, or LA. Rent for a one-bedroom runs $1,500-$2,200/month. No state income tax in Texas means your take-home pay stretches further. Food and entertainment are reasonable. The downside: Austin's cost of living has climbed 30%+ since 2020.

Why Get an MBA in Austin

Texas McCombs (#25) gives you a top-25 MBA in the middle of a tech boom. No state income tax means more money in your pocket. Austin's startup ecosystem is thriving, and the city's quality of life (live music, outdoors, food) is among the best in the country for young professionals.

What to Watch Out For

Austin summers are brutal (100+ degrees for months). Public transit is limited, so you'll need a car. McCombs is a large program, so you'll need to be proactive about building relationships and standing out in career services.

Frequently Asked Questions

What MBA programs are in Austin?

Texas McCombs (#25) at UT Austin is the city's MBA program. It's the highest-ranked MBA in Texas and one of the best values in top-25 MBA education, especially for Texas residents with in-state tuition for 2026.

Is Austin good for tech MBA careers?

Excellent. Tesla, Apple, Google, Amazon, Oracle, Dell, and hundreds of startups all have significant Austin presence. McCombs places 20%+ of graduates into tech. The city's tech scene is growing faster than almost any other US market.

How much does UT McCombs cost?

In-state tuition is approximately $40,000/year. Out-of-state tuition is approximately $56,000/year. Both are well below comparable top-25 programs. Add Austin's moderate cost of living, and McCombs is one of the best ROI stories in MBA education.
