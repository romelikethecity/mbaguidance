Honest ROI analysis for 2026

The Numbers

The all-in cost of Scranton (Kania) (tuition + living expenses + opportunity cost from an $80K salary) is approximately $264,000. The average starting salary of $68,000 produces an annual uplift of $-12,000 over the $80K baseline. At that rate, you break even in approximately 99 years.

When Scranton (Kania) Is Worth It

  • Career changers targeting Northeast PA Careers, Healthcare Management, Jesuit Education: Scranton (Kania)'s recruiting pipelines in these areas are well-established. If you're pivoting from a lower-paying industry, the salary uplift is significant.
  • Candidates with scholarship funding: A $50K-$100K scholarship dramatically improves ROI, reducing the breakeven by 1-2 years.
  • Targeting roles that require the credential: In consulting, banking, and PE, the top-150 MBA credential is a prerequisite, not a nice-to-have. Scranton (Kania)'s #150 ranking qualifies.

When It Might Not Be

  • Already earning $48,000+ in your target industry: If you're already near the post-MBA salary, the ROI depends on career acceleration rather than immediate salary uplift.
  • Taking on full debt at $44,000+ in tuition alone: High debt loads narrow your post-MBA choices. You may feel pressured to take the highest-paying offer rather than the best career fit.
  • Targeting industries where the MBA credential is optional: In entrepreneurship, some tech roles, and creative industries, the MBA provides network but not credential value. The ROI calculation shifts toward intangibles.

The Verdict

“Scranton (Kania) can be worth it for the right candidate. The key is whether the school's strengths in Northeast PA Careers, Healthcare Management, Jesuit Education align with your career goals and whether you can manage the cost through scholarships or in-state tuition. The $68,000 average salary produces an acceptable ROI for career changers coming from lower-paying roles.”
