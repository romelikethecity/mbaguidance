“Nashville is the healthcare capital of America and the music capital of the world. Belmont Massey sits at that intersection, connecting MBA students to an economy that's been one of the fastest-growing in the country for a decade.”

Program Overview

Nashville's economy is booming, driven by healthcare (HCA, Community Health Systems, Acadia Healthcare), music and entertainment (major labels, publishing, live music), and a tech sector that's growing faster than the city can build office space. Belmont University sits in the middle of Music Row, and the Massey MBA connects to both the healthcare capital and the entertainment economy.

The 30-student class gets entrepreneurship resources, Nashville employer access, and a campus culture that blends business with creativity. At $27K per year, the tuition is moderate for a private university in a growing market. Belmont's music school is world-class, and that creative DNA influences the business school culture. The Jack C. Massey naming honors the founder of HCA (Hospital Corporation of America), which reflects the program's healthcare roots.

Culture & Community

Belmont's campus is on Music Row, surrounded by recording studios, music publishers, and the landmarks of Nashville's entertainment industry. The culture is creative, entrepreneurial, and Nashville-proud. The 30-person class includes healthcare professionals, music industry workers, and entrepreneurs drawn to Nashville's opportunity. The city's legendary hospitality, food scene (hot chicken is non-negotiable), and live music add a lifestyle dimension. Nashville is one of the most exciting cities in America right now, and Belmont students feel it.

Academics & Curriculum

Healthcare management courses connect to Nashville's concentration of healthcare headquarters. Entrepreneurship resources include incubator space and mentorship from Nashville's startup community. Music and entertainment business courses are available through cross-registration. Core courses cover business fundamentals. Faculty bridge Nashville's healthcare and entertainment industries with academic business education.

Career Outcomes

HCA Healthcare, Community Health Systems, and Nashville's healthcare management companies provide the primary career pipeline. Music industry labels, publishers, and entertainment companies offer creative-industry paths. Nashville's growing tech and startup sectors expand the options. Average salary is approximately $82,000. The 83% employment rate reflects Nashville's strong job market. Career services uses the city's growth and employer relationships.

Who Should Apply

Nashville professionals who want to advance in the city's booming economy. Healthcare management candidates targeting the healthcare capital. Entertainment and music industry professionals. Entrepreneurs drawn to Nashville's startup energy.

What to Watch Out For

Belmont's MBA brand is eclipsed by Vanderbilt's Owen School in Nashville. At $27K per year, the tuition requires careful ROI calculation against the $82K salary. The brand is local to Nashville; outside the city, it's primarily known for music. If you're targeting national firms or industries without Nashville presence, the network doesn't extend. And Nashville's cost of living has risen sharply as the city grows.
