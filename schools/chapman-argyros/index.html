“Chapman's MBA sits in Orange County with a distinctive entertainment industry connection. The film school crossover (MBA/MFA dual degree) is unique in MBA education. For students building careers at the intersection of business and entertainment, this is the play.”

Program Overview

Chapman's Dodge College of Film and Media Arts is one of the top film schools in the country. The Argyros MBA benefits from that proximity with an MBA/MFA dual degree in Film and Television Producing that doesn't exist anywhere else. For students who want to run entertainment companies, produce content, or manage creative businesses, Chapman offers a combination that USC, UCLA, and NYU can't match at this level of integration.

The 28-student class gets a 16-month accelerated format with entrepreneurship emphasis and Orange County business connections. At $52K per year, the tuition is high for this tier. But the entertainment industry connection justifies the premium for students targeting that sector. Orange County's economy (tech, real estate, consumer goods) provides broader career options. The small class size means personalized attention from faculty and career services.

Culture & Community

Chapman's campus in the city of Orange is Southern California comfortable: palm trees, warm weather, and a downtown Orange historic district with restaurants and shops. The MBA culture is entrepreneurial and creative, influenced by the university's film school and performing arts programs. The 28-person class is tiny, which means everyone knows everyone. Students tend to be creative, ambitious, and drawn to the intersection of business and the arts. Orange County's lifestyle adds quality-of-life appeal.

Academics & Curriculum

The MBA/MFA in Film and Television Producing is the academic headline. Entrepreneurship courses are strong, supported by incubator resources and mentorship. Core business courses cover fundamentals in the 16-month accelerated timeline. Orange County's tech and consumer goods companies provide case studies and project partners. Faculty bridge business education and the creative industries. The accelerated format means the pace is intense but the opportunity cost is lower.

Career Outcomes

Entertainment companies (studios, production companies, agencies), Orange County tech firms, consumer goods companies, and the real estate sector provide career options. Average salary is approximately $76,000. The 80% employment rate reflects the competitive Southern California market. Chapman's entertainment connections extend to LA's creative industry ecosystem. Career services is personalized in the small program. Entrepreneurship-minded graduates launch ventures with incubator support.

Who Should Apply

Entertainment industry professionals who want business credentials. Entrepreneurs in Southern California. Creative professionals seeking the MBA/MFA combination. Anyone drawn to an intimate accelerated program in Orange County.

What to Watch Out For

At $52K per year, the tuition is steep for a program outside the top 100. The $76K average salary makes the ROI challenging without scholarship support. Chapman's MBA brand is local to Southern California; outside the region and the entertainment industry, it's not widely recognized. If you're targeting traditional business careers in finance, consulting, or tech, Chapman's positioning doesn't add value over less expensive alternatives.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the acceptance rate at Chapman Argyros MBA?

Chapman Argyros admits approximately 55% of MBA applicants for the class of 2026. The program enrolls about 28 students in its 16-month accelerated format.

Does Chapman offer an MBA/MFA dual degree?

Yes. Chapman's MBA/MFA in Film and Television Producing combines the Argyros MBA with the Dodge College film school. This dual degree is unique in MBA education, designed for students building careers at the intersection of business and entertainment.

What is the average salary after Chapman Argyros MBA?

Chapman Argyros MBA graduates earn an average starting salary of approximately $76,000. Entertainment, entrepreneurship, real estate, and consumer goods roles in Southern California drive placement.
