“$15K a year for a top-80 MBA. SUNY Buffalo is the cheapest path to a ranked business degree in the Northeast.”

Program Overview

SUNY Buffalo charges $15,230 per year for in-state students. That's less than one semester at most private MBA programs. The School of Management is AACSB-accredited, consistently ranked in the top 80, and produces graduates who enter finance, healthcare administration, and consulting. For New York State residents, the math is hard to argue with: a ranked MBA for the cost of a used car.

The 60-student class works in cohort study groups of 5-6 students, creating collaboration structures within the larger program. Healthcare management is a growing specialty, driven by Buffalo's large hospital systems and health services economy. Finance and MIS round out the academic strengths. Buffalo's cost of living is among the lowest of any city with a ranked MBA program, which means your student budget stretches further here than almost anywhere else.

Culture & Community

Buffalo's culture is blue-collar, loyal, and underestimated. The city has been in a renaissance phase, with investment in downtown, the waterfront, and the food scene. SUNY Buffalo students benefit from that revitalization alongside the university's large research campus. The MBA program attracts a mix of career changers and early-career professionals drawn by the price point. The cohort study group system builds teamwork skills in a practical, no-nonsense environment.

Academics & Curriculum

Finance, healthcare management, and MIS form the academic core. The Bloomberg terminal lab provides hands-on financial modeling experience. Healthcare administration courses connect to Buffalo's hospital systems and health services employers. The curriculum follows a traditional two-year MBA structure with elective specialization in the second year. Faculty are accessible and research-active, with particular strength in financial economics and health systems management.

Career Outcomes

Healthcare administration, finance, and consulting are the primary placement sectors. Buffalo's large hospital networks (Kaleida Health, Catholic Health) are significant employers. Financial services firms in the region and in New York City recruit from the program. The $75,000 average salary is the lowest in this tier but must be evaluated against the $15K tuition and Buffalo's low cost of living. For in-state students, the total MBA investment is under $50,000, making the payback period remarkably short.

Who Should Apply

New York State residents who want the cheapest ranked MBA available. Healthcare professionals targeting administration roles. Budget-conscious candidates who prioritize ROI over brand prestige. Anyone who wants a solid business education without six figures of student debt.

What to Watch Out For

The SUNY Buffalo MBA brand is regional. Outside New York State, the name competes with many other state university MBAs. The $75K average salary is low for a ranked program, though the cost-of-living context matters. Buffalo's winters are legendary for a reason, and the local job market, while improving, is smaller than what you'd find in Boston, New York City, or Philadelphia. If you want a national MBA brand, SUNY Buffalo isn't it.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the acceptance rate at SUNY Buffalo MBA?

SUNY Buffalo admits approximately 66% of MBA applicants for the class of 2026. The program enrolls about 60 students.

How much does SUNY Buffalo MBA cost?

SUNY Buffalo MBA costs approximately $15,230 per year for in-state students and $27,530 for out-of-state students. It's the most affordable ranked MBA in the Northeast.

What is the average salary after SUNY Buffalo MBA?

SUNY Buffalo MBA graduates earn an average starting salary of approximately $75,000. Healthcare administration, finance, and consulting roles drive placement. The low tuition makes the ROI strong despite the modest salary figure.
