“The University of Scranton Kania offers a Jesuit MBA in northeastern Pennsylvania with healthcare management and accounting strengths. The program is personal, values-driven, and connected to the region's healthcare economy.”

Program Overview

Scranton is a former coal city in northeastern Pennsylvania that's reinvented itself around healthcare, education, and logistics. The University of Scranton's Kania MBA connects to that regional economy with healthcare management and accounting concentrations. The Jesuit tradition adds ethical leadership and service to the business education. At $22K per year, the tuition is moderate for a private Jesuit university.

The 25-student class gets individual attention from faculty in a university that takes teaching seriously. Healthcare systems (Geisinger, Commonwealth Health) and regional employers provide the career backdrop. The program serves working professionals with flexible scheduling. Scranton's cost of living is among the lowest in the Northeast, which stretches both the educational investment and the post-MBA salary.

Culture & Community

Scranton's Jesuit tradition shapes a community that values service, ethics, and personal development. The 25-person class is intimate and supportive. The city has a blue-collar heritage that's evolving (The Office put Scranton on the cultural map). The university campus is attractive, and the cost of living makes student life comfortable. Students who value the Jesuit approach to education and a personal MBA experience will find Kania's culture rewarding.

Academics & Curriculum

Healthcare management courses build on the region's hospital systems and health services economy. Accounting courses connect to the Kania School's strong undergraduate accounting program. Core business courses cover fundamentals with ethical leadership woven throughout. The Jesuit emphasis on intellectual rigor and moral development shapes the academic culture. Faculty are accessible and invested in student outcomes. The small program provides a thorough, personal business education.

Career Outcomes

Regional healthcare systems (Geisinger, Commonwealth Health), accounting firms, and northeastern PA businesses provide career options. Average salary is approximately $68,000. The 78% employment rate reflects the regional market. Career services provides personalized support. The Jesuit alumni network connects to other Jesuit university graduates across the Northeast. For healthcare management and accounting careers in the region, the connections are solid.

Who Should Apply

Northeastern PA professionals targeting local career advancement. Healthcare management candidates connected to the region's hospital systems. Accounting professionals who want MBA credentials. Anyone drawn to the Jesuit approach to business education.

What to Watch Out For

The Scranton Kania brand is hyperlocal. Outside northeastern PA and Jesuit circles, it doesn't carry weight in MBA recruiting. The $68K salary is low by MBA standards. Scranton's job market is small, and many graduates need to look to NYC, Philadelphia, or other metros for career options. If you need a nationally recognized MBA credential or access to major employer pipelines, this program doesn't provide that. It works for regional career advancement, not national mobility.
