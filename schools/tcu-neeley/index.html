Program Overview

TCU Neeley's outcomes data doesn't match its ranking. $120,000 average total compensation. 96% employment rate. Faculty ranked #1 in the world by The Economist three years running. Those numbers belong to a top-40 program, and they come from a school that most MBA applicants overlook. The STEM-designated curriculum provides quantitative rigor and international student OPT benefits. The DFW metro, with corporate headquarters for American Airlines, BNSF Railway, Lockheed Martin, and dozens of Fortune 500 companies, provides the employer base.

The 45-student class receives an integrated curriculum where courses build on each other rather than operating as isolated modules. Entrepreneurship is the flagship strength, supported by the Neeley Entrepreneurship Center and connections to Fort Worth's growing startup scene. Supply chain management, finance, and analytics round out the academic strengths. The campus in Fort Worth offers Texas hospitality, a lower cost of living than Dallas, and easy access to the broader DFW job market.

Culture & Community

TCU's culture blends Southern hospitality with serious academic ambition. The 45-person class creates a collaborative environment where competition is with the market, not each other. Fort Worth is the "City of Cowboys and Culture," with a world-class museum district, a growing food scene, and housing costs that look absurd to anyone from the coasts. The Christian university heritage is present but not dominant in the business school. Students who want professional polish with personal warmth will find TCU's culture appealing.

Academics & Curriculum

The integrated curriculum design is the academic differentiator. Core courses are sequenced to build on each other, creating connections across disciplines that modular curricula miss. Entrepreneurship coursework through the Neeley Entrepreneurship Center includes venture competitions, mentorship programs, and startup incubation. Supply chain management benefits from DFW's logistics infrastructure. The STEM designation adds quantitative depth. Faculty quality, ranked #1 globally by The Economist, means classroom instruction is a genuine competitive advantage.

Career Outcomes

DFW's corporate concentration drives placement. American Airlines, BNSF, Lockheed Martin, and dozens of Fortune 500 companies headquartered in the metroplex recruit from Neeley. Consulting (25%), finance (20%), and supply chain/operations (18%) are the top sectors. The $120,000 average total compensation (salary plus bonus) places Neeley's outcomes alongside programs ranked in the top 40-50. The 96% employment rate is exceptional for a program of this size. DFW's diversified economy provides career opportunities across every major industry.

Who Should Apply

Professionals targeting DFW's corporate job market. Entrepreneurs who want world-class faculty and startup resources. Anyone attracted to STEM-designated MBA outcomes at a school that outperforms its ranking. Candidates who value teaching quality and career outcomes over brand prestige alone.

What to Watch Out For

The TCU Neeley brand is strongest in Texas. Outside the state, the program competes for recognition against schools with larger alumni networks and more national marketing. Fort Worth, while growing, is sometimes overshadowed by Dallas in recruiting conversations. The $46K annual tuition is moderate for outcomes in this range but high for a Texas school. If you want a coast-based career after graduation, TCU's geographic advantages don't translate directly.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the acceptance rate at TCU Neeley MBA?

TCU Neeley admits approximately 50% of MBA applicants for the class of 2026. The STEM-designated program enrolls about 45 students.

Is TCU Neeley MBA STEM-designated?

Yes. TCU Neeley's MBA carries a STEM designation, providing international students with extended OPT work authorization. The curriculum integrates quantitative analysis, analytics, and data-driven decision-making throughout the core.

What is the average salary after TCU Neeley MBA?

TCU Neeley MBA graduates earn average total compensation of approximately $120,000 (salary plus bonus). The 96% employment rate and DFW's Fortune 500 concentration drive strong placement across consulting, finance, and supply chain roles.
