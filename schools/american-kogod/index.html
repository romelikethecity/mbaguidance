“An MBA in the nation's capital with sustainability and public policy woven into the curriculum. American Kogod trains business leaders who understand that government is the biggest customer in the room.”

Program Overview

Washington DC is the one city where business, government, and international policy all meet in the same buildings. American Kogod's MBA is built for that intersection. Sustainability coursework, government consulting projects, and international business programs draw on the city's unique position. The World Bank, IMF, State Department, and hundreds of trade associations and international organizations are within commuting distance. No other MBA program has this particular proximity advantage.

The 45-student class gets a 21-month MBA with concentrations in sustainability, consulting, and international business alongside traditional finance and marketing tracks. American University's School of International Service (one of the top IR schools in the country) provides cross-registration opportunities. The DC location means that guest speakers are frequently senior government officials, World Bank economists, or heads of international trade organizations. At $48K per year, the tuition carries a DC premium, but the access to the policy and international business world is the value proposition.

Culture & Community

American Kogod's culture is policy-aware, international, and mission-oriented. DC attracts people who care about how the world works, and that ethos shapes the MBA student body. The 45-person class includes international students, career diplomats transitioning to business, and professionals from government agencies seeking private-sector skills. The university's Massachusetts Avenue campus is in one of DC's most established neighborhoods. Students who want an MBA with a global and policy perspective will find Kogod's culture stimulating.

Academics & Curriculum

Sustainability is the academic signature. Kogod's Net Impact chapter is consistently among the most active in the country. Government consulting projects provide experiential learning with federal agencies and international organizations. International business courses build on DC's diplomatic and trade community. Cross-registration with the School of International Service creates combinations (MBA plus international affairs) that are unique to American. Finance and marketing concentrations serve students targeting traditional business roles.

Career Outcomes

DC's unique employer landscape drives placement. Government consulting firms (Deloitte, Booz Allen Hamilton, Accenture Federal Services), international organizations (World Bank, IMF), and policy-focused companies recruit from Kogod. Consulting (25%), government/policy (20%), finance (15%), and sustainability/CSR (12%) are the top sectors. Average salary is approximately $85,000. The DC alumni network opens doors in government affairs, international business, and sustainability roles that other MBA programs can't easily access.

Who Should Apply

Professionals targeting careers at the intersection of business and government. Sustainability-focused candidates who want policy-level impact. International business professionals drawn to DC's diplomatic community. Consultants targeting federal practice areas. Anyone who wants an MBA where "stakeholder" includes sovereign governments and international institutions.

What to Watch Out For

The American Kogod brand is strongest in DC. Outside the capital, the name competes with many other private university MBAs. The $85K average salary reflects DC's mix of government and private sector compensation, which trails pure private sector salaries in finance and tech hubs. The $48K tuition is a premium for outcomes in this tier. If your career goals are in pure corporate finance, tech, or industries with no government interface, Kogod's positioning doesn't add value. DC's cost of living is high and rising.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the acceptance rate at American Kogod MBA?

American Kogod admits approximately 55% of MBA applicants for the class of 2026. The program enrolls about 45 students.

Is American Kogod good for sustainability careers?

American Kogod's sustainability programs are among the most active of any MBA. The DC location provides access to policy organizations, international institutions, and government agencies that shape sustainability standards and regulations. Net Impact rankings consistently recognize Kogod's social impact programming.

What is the average salary after American Kogod MBA?

American Kogod MBA graduates earn an average starting salary of approximately $85,000. Government consulting, international organizations, finance, and sustainability roles in the DC metro drive placement outcomes.
