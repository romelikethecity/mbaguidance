“An affordable Jesuit MBA in the motor capital. Detroit Mercy connects graduates to the automotive ecosystem that still drives Michigan's economy, with a values-based education that costs less than most private alternatives.”

Program Overview

The University of Detroit Mercy sits in a city that's rebuilding itself. The MBA program connects to that reinvention through automotive industry partnerships, a growing tech startup scene, and the manufacturing base that still anchors Michigan's economy. The Jesuit and Mercy traditions emphasize ethical leadership, service, and social responsibility. At $28K per year, it's one of the more affordable private MBAs in the Midwest.

The 30-student class gets personalized attention and faculty who know every student by name. Half-tuition scholarships are available for students scoring 600+ on the GMAT, which drops the net cost to public university levels. The program's evening format accommodates working professionals, and Detroit's cost of living is among the lowest of any major metro. GM, Ford, and Stellantis all have headquarters within commuting distance.

Culture & Community

Detroit Mercy's culture reflects the city: scrappy, resilient, and unpretentious. The Jesuit tradition adds an ethics dimension that shows up in coursework and community service requirements. The 30-person class creates tight bonds. Students tend to be working professionals with roots in Metro Detroit. The campus is urban, and the city's renaissance (new restaurants, tech investment, sports facilities) adds energy that wasn't there a decade ago.

Academics & Curriculum

The curriculum covers core business fundamentals with concentrations in finance, marketing, and management. The Jesuit emphasis on ethics runs through multiple courses. Automotive industry case studies appear frequently given the location. Evening classes accommodate working professionals. The small class size means faculty are accessible and engaged. No specialized tech or analytics concentration exists, so students seeking data science depth should look elsewhere.

Career Outcomes

GM, Ford, Stellantis, and their supplier networks are the primary employer pipeline. Finance, consulting, and healthcare round out the placement picture. Average salary is approximately $73,000, reflecting Detroit's lower cost of living. The alumni network is loyal and concentrated in Southeast Michigan. Career services provides individual coaching in a small program. If your career goals are in Metro Detroit, the connections are strong.

Who Should Apply

Detroit-area professionals who want to advance without relocating. Automotive industry workers seeking business credentials. Anyone who values a Jesuit education's emphasis on ethics and service. Budget-conscious students who can earn the half-tuition GMAT scholarship.

What to Watch Out For

The Detroit Mercy brand is hyper-local. Outside Michigan, it competes with dozens of small private MBAs for recognition. The $73K average salary is modest by MBA standards, though it stretches further in Detroit's low-cost market. If your career goals are in tech, finance hubs, or consulting, the program doesn't have recruiting pipelines to those markets.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the acceptance rate at Detroit Mercy MBA?

Detroit Mercy admits approximately 85% of MBA applicants for the class of 2026. The program enrolls about 30 students.

Does Detroit Mercy offer MBA scholarships?

Detroit Mercy offers half-tuition scholarships for MBA applicants scoring 600+ on the GMAT. This brings the annual cost to approximately $14,000, making it competitive with in-state public university tuition.

What is the average salary after Detroit Mercy MBA?

Detroit Mercy MBA graduates earn an average starting salary of approximately $73,000. Automotive, finance, and consulting roles in Metro Detroit drive placement.
