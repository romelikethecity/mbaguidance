Program Overview

Denver Daniels operates with a simple financial proposition: small class, generous scholarships, and a city economy that's been on a decade-long growth streak. Over 80% of MBA students receive scholarship awards, some up to full tuition. The 40-student cohort gets personalized attention in a city where tech companies, energy firms, and real estate developers are all expanding their operations and their hiring.

Ethics and sustainability are woven into the curriculum in a way that goes beyond a single elective. The Daniels Fund Ethics Initiative provides case studies, competitions, and guest speakers focused on ethical decision-making. Real estate and construction management courses build on Denver's building boom. The $100,000 average salary and strong alumni salary growth ($120K median at four months out) demonstrate that the program's outcomes justify the investment, especially when scholarship money reduces the sticker price.

Culture & Community

Denver Daniels attracts students who want career growth in a city they want to live in. Denver's outdoor lifestyle (skiing, hiking, 300 days of sunshine) is part of the recruiting pitch, and it works. The 40-person class bonds through small-group projects and Denver's social scene. The ethics focus creates a culture where conversations about values are natural, not forced. Students who want to stay in Colorado after graduation find a strong alumni network in the Denver metro.

Academics & Curriculum

Ethics integration is the academic identity. Every core course includes ethical dimensions, and the Daniels Fund Ethics Initiative provides programming throughout the two years. Real estate and construction management courses are distinctive strengths, reflecting Denver's building economy. Finance, entrepreneurship, and sustainability round out the concentration options. The 21-month format includes a summer internship. Core courses average 25 students, smaller than most MBA programs.

Career Outcomes

Denver-based employers in tech, real estate, energy, and consulting are the primary recruiters. Finance (22%), consulting (18%), real estate (15%), and tech (20%) are the top sectors. The $100,000 average salary reflects Denver's strong compensation levels. Alumni report $120,000 median base salary at four months post-graduation, suggesting that the initial placement figures understate the program's career impact. Denver's growing tech scene (Oracle, Google, Amazon all have expanding Denver offices) adds employer diversity.

Who Should Apply

Professionals who want to live and work in Denver. Real estate professionals seeking MBA credentials in a booming market. Ethics-focused candidates who want values integration throughout the curriculum. Anyone attracted to a small cohort with generous scholarship support in a top-tier quality-of-life city.

What to Watch Out For

The $48K annual sticker price is high for this tier, though scholarships reduce the actual cost for most students. The Daniels brand is strongest in Colorado. If you want to work in New York, Chicago, or the Bay Area after graduation, you'll be competing against candidates from programs with stronger national reach. Denver's cost of living has risen sharply, which partially offsets the salary advantage. And the 40-person class, while intimate, limits the breadth of your in-program network.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the acceptance rate at Denver Daniels MBA?

Denver Daniels admits approximately 65% of MBA applicants for the class of 2026. The program enrolls about 40 students per cohort.

Do Denver Daniels MBA students get scholarships?

Yes. Over 80% of Denver Daniels MBA students receive scholarship awards, with some receiving up to full tuition. The school is committed to making the MBA accessible, and scholarship consideration is automatic with admission.

What is the average salary after Denver Daniels MBA?

Denver Daniels MBA graduates earn an average starting salary of approximately $100,000, with alumni reporting $120,000 median base salary at four months post-graduation. Tech, real estate, consulting, and finance roles in the Denver metro drive the strongest outcomes.
