Program Overview

UAB Medical Center is one of the largest academic medical centers in the country, and UAB itself is Birmingham's largest employer. That healthcare ecosystem shapes the Collat MBA in ways that a standalone business school can't replicate. Healthcare management coursework connects to a hospital system that's pioneering research, treating patients, and training physicians on the same campus where MBA students take classes.

The 35-student class gets concentrations in healthcare management, IT management, and finance at $16,200 per year for in-state students. A one-year accelerated option is available for students with business undergraduate degrees. Birmingham's cost of living is among the lowest of any major metro, which stretches the budget. The program serves working professionals with evening classes and online options.

Culture & Community

Birmingham is reinventing itself. The city's food scene, arts district, and startup community have grown significantly over the past decade. UAB's culture reflects that energy: ambitious, practical, and aware of the city's progress. The 35-person class includes healthcare workers, engineers, and professionals from the region's banking and manufacturing sectors. The university's research hospital adds a seriousness to the campus that pure business schools lack.

Academics & Curriculum

Healthcare management courses are the academic centerpiece, drawing on UAB Medical Center's resources and case studies. IT management and finance concentrations serve broader career goals. The one-year option condenses the program for students with business backgrounds. Faculty bring healthcare research credentials and industry connections. The curriculum is practical, focused on skills that Birmingham's employer base values.

Career Outcomes

UAB Health System, Regions Financial, Protective Life, and Southern Company are major employers. Healthcare administration and management roles are the primary pipeline. Average salary is approximately $75,000. Birmingham's affordable cost of living means the $75K salary provides a comfortable lifestyle. Career services connects students to the regional healthcare and finance sectors. The UAB alumni network in Alabama is extensive.

Who Should Apply

Healthcare professionals who want business credentials from a program embedded in a major medical center. Alabama residents who want affordable, high-quality education. Working professionals who need evening or online flexibility. Career changers targeting healthcare management.

What to Watch Out For

Birmingham's job market is smaller than Atlanta, Nashville, or other Southern hubs. The UAB brand is strong in Alabama but less recognized outside the state, except in healthcare circles. The $75K salary is modest, though Birmingham's cost of living makes it stretch. If your goals are in tech, consulting, or industries concentrated in larger metros, UAB's pipelines are limited.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the acceptance rate at UAB Collat MBA?

UAB Collat admits approximately 75% of MBA applicants for the class of 2026. The program enrolls about 35 students.

Is UAB Collat good for healthcare careers?

UAB Collat's healthcare management specialization is connected to UAB Medical Center, one of the nation's largest academic medical centers. The proximity to a research hospital and Birmingham's healthcare economy creates career opportunities in healthcare administration and management.

What is the average salary after UAB Collat MBA?

UAB Collat MBA graduates earn an average starting salary of approximately $75,000. Healthcare, finance, and IT management roles in Birmingham drive placement. At $16,200/year in-state tuition, the ROI is strong.
