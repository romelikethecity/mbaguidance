Program Overview

New Rochelle is in Westchester County, 30 minutes north of Manhattan by Metro-North train. Westchester is home to PepsiCo, Mastercard, IBM's Watson headquarters, and dozens of Fortune 500 companies' suburban campuses. Iona LaPenta's MBA connects to that corporate base at $25K per year, which is moderate for a private school in the NYC suburbs.

The 30-student class gets a program designed for Westchester working professionals. Finance and management concentrations serve the local employer needs. Evening and weekend classes accommodate careers. The school attained university status in 2022 (from Iona College), signaling institutional growth. The Edmund Rice Christian Brothers tradition adds a values dimension. For Westchester professionals who want business credentials without commuting to the city, LaPenta provides a local option.

Culture & Community

Iona's campus is suburban Westchester: tree-lined, comfortable, and close to the Long Island Sound. The MBA culture is professional and flexible, serving working adults who commute from across the county. The 30-person class includes finance professionals, healthcare workers, and corporate employees from Westchester's Fortune 500 campuses. The culture is practical and career-focused. New Rochelle's downtown is revitalizing, with new restaurants and cultural attractions.

Academics & Curriculum

Finance courses are the academic strength, aligned with Westchester's corporate base. Management and marketing concentrations serve broader career goals. Evening and weekend scheduling accommodates working professionals. The curriculum is practical, focused on skills that regional employers value. Faculty connect to the Westchester business community. The program is straightforward: core business education for career advancement.

Career Outcomes

Westchester's Fortune 500 campuses (PepsiCo, Mastercard, IBM), NYC commutable employers, and regional healthcare systems drive placement. Average salary is approximately $85,000. The 80% employment rate reflects the NYC metro's competitive market. Career services uses the Westchester corporate base. The Iona alumni network is concentrated in the northern suburbs and lower Hudson Valley.

Who Should Apply

Westchester professionals who want to advance without commuting to Manhattan for classes. Finance and corporate professionals at suburban campuses. Working adults who need evening and weekend flexibility. Anyone who values a values-driven education in the NYC suburbs.

What to Watch Out For

The Iona brand is local to Westchester and the NYC metro. In competitive recruiting, it's outshone by NYU Stern, Columbia, and several other city programs. The $85K salary is modest for the metro area's cost of living. If you're targeting career-changing roles at brand-name firms, Iona's credential doesn't carry the weight. The program works for advancement within existing careers, not for launching into new industries.
