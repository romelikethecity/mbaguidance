“A STEM-designated MBA sitting inside Research Triangle Park. NC State Jenkins turns engineers into business leaders for $28K a year.”

Program Overview

NC State Jenkins sits in the middle of Research Triangle Park, one of the largest research and technology hubs in the country. Over 125 organizations operate in the Triangle, including major operations from Cisco, IBM, SAS, and dozens of biotech companies. The MBA program was built to serve that ecosystem. Technology management, business analytics, and innovation management are the academic anchors, and the STEM designation reflects the quantitative rigor embedded in the curriculum.

The 47-student cohort is small enough for personalized attention from faculty who hold active research partnerships with Triangle companies. The Center for Innovation Management Studies connects MBA students to real consulting projects with corporate sponsors. At $28K in-state tuition for the full 21-month program, the cost-to-outcome ratio is competitive with any public MBA in the Southeast. Raleigh's booming tech economy provides a strong local job market, and the Triangle's livability scores rank among the best in the country.

Culture & Community

NC State's culture runs on collaboration and applied problem-solving. The engineering DNA of the university shapes the business school's approach: build things, test them, iterate. The 47-person cohort bonds through the Technology Entrepreneurship Commercialization program, where students take real inventions from NC State's labs and develop go-to-market strategies. Raleigh is affordable, walkable, and growing fast. The food scene punches above its weight.

Academics & Curriculum

The STEM designation means the curriculum has a quantitative foundation that goes beyond standard MBA analytics. Innovation management, technology entrepreneurship, and supply chain courses build on Research Triangle partnerships. Faculty research feeds directly into classroom content. The 21-month format includes a summer internship, and the small class size means you're working directly with professors, not teaching assistants.

Career Outcomes

Technology and analytics companies in the Research Triangle are the primary employers. Cisco, SAS, IBM, and biotech firms recruit from Jenkins. Consulting (18%), tech (28%), and healthcare/pharma (15%) are the top sectors. The $101K average salary reflects the Triangle's compensation levels, which are strong but below the Bay Area or NYC. Partnerships with 200+ corporations through the Center for Innovation Management Studies create a pipeline that smaller programs can't replicate.

Who Should Apply

Engineers and technologists who want to move into management. Professionals targeting the Research Triangle's tech and biotech ecosystem. Analytics-minded candidates who want a STEM-designated MBA at a public school price. Career changers who value applied learning over case-study theory.

What to Watch Out For

The Jenkins brand is regional. Outside the Triangle and Southeast, the name requires context. If your career goals point to New York finance or Bay Area tech, you'll be competing against candidates from programs with stronger national recruiting pipelines. The $101K average salary is solid for Raleigh's cost of living but trails programs with comparable rankings in higher-cost metros. And Raleigh, while growing fast, is still a mid-size city.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the acceptance rate at NC State Jenkins MBA?

NC State Jenkins admits approximately 48% of MBA applicants for the class of 2026. The STEM-designated program enrolls about 47 students per cohort.

Is NC State Jenkins MBA STEM-designated?

Yes. The Jenkins MBA carries a STEM designation, which provides international students with an extended 36-month OPT work authorization period. The curriculum integrates technology management, analytics, and innovation throughout the core.

What is the average salary after NC State Jenkins MBA?

Jenkins MBA graduates earn an average starting salary of approximately $101,000. Research Triangle Park employers in tech, biotech, and consulting drive the strongest placement outcomes.
