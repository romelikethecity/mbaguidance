Program Overview

Memphis is a logistics city. FedEx's global superhub at Memphis International Airport processes 15 million packages on peak nights. International Paper, AutoZone, and a massive distribution infrastructure make the city a supply chain laboratory. Fogelman's MBA supply chain program is built on that foundation, with faculty research, company partnerships, and internship pipelines that connect directly to the industry moving through the city.

At $12,300 per year for in-state students, the tuition is among the lowest of any AACSB-accredited MBA. The 35-student class gets a program that punches above its ranking in supply chain education. Memphis's cost of living is low, and the $92K average salary creates a strong ROI. The program serves both full-time students and working professionals with evening options.

Culture & Community

Memphis is blues, barbecue, and business. The city's cultural identity is strong and unpretentious. The MBA culture reflects that: practical, diverse, and connected to the working economy. The 35-person class includes logistics professionals, FedEx employees, and career changers from across the Mid-South. The university is one of Memphis's largest employers, and the campus community is active. Students drawn to Memphis's distinct culture and affordable lifestyle will find the environment rewarding.

Academics & Curriculum

Supply chain management courses cover logistics, procurement, operations, and distribution network design. The FedEx connection provides case studies, guest speakers, and internship opportunities. Finance and marketing concentrations serve broader career goals. The AACSB-accredited curriculum is rigorous for the price point. Faculty bring research in supply chain optimization and logistics technology. The program is strongest where Memphis is strongest: moving goods efficiently.

Career Outcomes

FedEx, International Paper, AutoZone, and the broader logistics industry are the primary employer pipeline. Consulting firms and healthcare companies round out the options. Average salary is approximately $92,000. The 83% employment rate reflects strong demand for supply chain talent. Memphis's distribution economy means graduates can stay local with excellent career prospects. The program's supply chain reputation extends nationally within the industry.

Who Should Apply

Supply chain and logistics professionals who want business leadership credentials. Value-conscious students who want maximum ROI. Working professionals in Memphis's distribution economy. Anyone building a career in supply chain management, procurement, or operations.

What to Watch Out For

Memphis's job market is concentrated in logistics, healthcare, and a few other sectors. If your career goals don't involve supply chain, the program's unique advantages don't apply. The Fogelman brand is strongest in the Mid-South and supply chain circles. Outside those contexts, it's a mid-tier public university MBA. Memphis's economic challenges (poverty rate, crime statistics) are real, though they don't directly affect the MBA experience.
