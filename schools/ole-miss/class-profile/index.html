Ole Miss's class of 35 students represents a small, intimate community. The smaller class creates tight-knit relationships and a community where you'll know most of your classmates by name.

Academic Profile

The average GMAT score is 532, with the middle 80% typically ranging from 502 to 552. The average GPA is 3.4. These are averages, meaning roughly half the admitted class scored below these numbers. Strong work experience, compelling essays, and demonstrated leadership can offset below-average test scores.

Most admitted students have 3-7 years of work experience, with an average of approximately 5 years. The admissions committee looks for candidates who've had enough professional experience to contribute meaningfully to classroom discussions but are early enough in their careers to benefit from the MBA's career-changing potential.

Professional Background

Ole Miss attracts students from diverse professional backgrounds. Common pre-MBA industries include consulting, financial services, technology, and non-profit/government. The school particularly values candidates whose backgrounds align with its strengths in Online MBA, Palmer Assistantships, Value, SEC Network.

Diversity of background is a deliberate admissions strategy. Schools build classes where a former investment banker sits next to a Peace Corps volunteer sits next to a software engineer. This diversity is the source of the MBA classroom's value.

What This Means for Applicants

Understanding the class profile helps you position your application. If your GMAT is below 532, consider retaking or emphasizing other strengths. If you come from an over-represented background (consulting, banking), you'll need to differentiate on leadership, personal story, and school fit. If you come from an under-represented background (military, non-profit, healthcare), that diversity is an asset.

The class profile also tells you what your learning environment will look like. A class of 35 with these academic credentials means a collaborative, supportive environment that values practical experience.

Ole Miss MBA Class of 2026: Class Size and Average Work Experience Summary

For the Ole Miss MBA Class of 2026, the headline numbers are: class size of 35 students, average GMAT of 532, average GPA of 3.4, and approximately 5 years of average work experience. The middle 80% GMAT range typically falls between 502 and 552. Most students enter with 3-7 years of professional experience.

Class size of 35 matters because it shapes everything about the experience: the intimacy of class discussions, the breadth of the alumni network, and how often you cross paths with the same classmates. A small class size means tight community bonds and direct access to faculty and admissions, with a more intimate alumni network at the cost of some recruiting breadth.

Five years of average work experience reflects the Ole Miss admissions philosophy of admitting candidates with enough professional context to contribute meaningfully but early enough in their careers to benefit from the MBA pivot. Candidates with two or fewer years rarely succeed without unusually strong leadership or entrepreneurial accomplishments. Candidates with eight-plus years often face questions about the cost-benefit of a traditional MBA versus an Executive MBA program.
