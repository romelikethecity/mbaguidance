Program Overview

Clark University has a long history of social engagement, dating back to its founding as the first all-graduate university in the US. The MBA program carries that tradition forward with concentrations in social change management, international development, and sustainability. These aren't token offerings: they're the program's identity. Students come to Clark because they want to use business as a tool for social impact.

Worcester is Massachusetts' second-largest city, 40 miles west of Boston. It's affordable by Massachusetts standards, with a growing innovation economy anchored by biotech, healthcare, and higher education. The 30-student class gets individual attention in a university that values intellectual curiosity and social engagement. At $31K per year, the tuition is a premium for outcomes in this tier, but the social change focus attracts students for whom ROI is measured in impact, not just salary.

Culture & Community

Clark's culture is intellectual, progressive, and mission-driven. The university attracts students who care about the world's problems and want business tools to address them. The 30-person class includes international students, nonprofit professionals, and career changers from social impact backgrounds. Worcester's Main South neighborhood, where Clark is located, provides real-world social enterprise context. The culture is earnest and engaged. Students who want a traditional corporate MBA experience will feel out of place.

Academics & Curriculum

Social change management courses address nonprofit management, social enterprise, and community development. International development coursework covers emerging market economics, microfinance, and global health systems. Sustainability concentrations integrate environmental and social considerations. Core business courses provide the fundamental toolkit. Faculty bring deep expertise in social enterprise and development economics. The curriculum is designed for students who want to change the world through business.

Career Outcomes

Nonprofit organizations, international development agencies (USAID, World Bank), social enterprises, and healthcare companies provide career options. Average salary is approximately $63,000, reflecting the social impact and nonprofit sector's lower compensation. The 75% employment rate trails most peer programs. Career services supports students targeting mission-driven organizations. The Clark alumni network is concentrated in social enterprise and international development circles.

Who Should Apply

Social enterprise builders who want business credentials. International development professionals targeting management roles. Nonprofit leaders who need business skills. Students who measure success in impact, not just salary.

What to Watch Out For

The $63K average salary is low by MBA standards, and the 75% employment rate is below average. At $31K per year, the ROI is challenging for debt-financed students. The Clark MBA brand is niche; outside social enterprise and development circles, it doesn't carry weight. If you're targeting corporate careers, consulting, or finance, Clark's positioning actively works against you. And Worcester, while improving, is not Boston.
