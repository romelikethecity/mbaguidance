“A 30-person MBA cohort an hour from Boston. New Hampshire Paul offers the personalized attention of a small program with access to New England's economy. Not glamorous, but for career changers who need individual coaching, the size is the feature.”

Program Overview

Durham, New Hampshire is a small college town an hour north of Boston. UNH's Paul College MBA trades big-city buzz for small-class intimacy: 30 students per cohort, faculty who know your name and career goals, and an environment where every student gets individual attention. For career changers who need coaching through a pivot, that personalization matters more than prestige.

New England's economy provides the career backdrop. Boston's financial services, biotech, and tech companies are commutable for internships and networking. New Hampshire's own business-friendly environment (no state income tax or sales tax) attracts companies looking for Northeast talent at lower costs. At $20K per year for in-state students, the program is affordable. The 90% acceptance rate makes it accessible for candidates who might not get into more selective programs.

Culture & Community

UNH's Durham campus is classic New England: red brick, tree-lined paths, and proximity to the New Hampshire seacoast. The MBA culture is intimate and supportive. With 30 students, there's no hiding in the back row. The town is small, which means social life centers on the cohort, local restaurants, and outdoor activities (skiing, hiking, coastal access). Students drawn to small-town New England and close-knit communities will thrive. Students who want big-city energy will feel the limits.

Academics & Curriculum

The curriculum covers core business fundamentals with flexibility to tailor electives to individual career goals. Small class sizes mean faculty can adjust teaching to the cohort's needs. Finance, marketing, and management concentrations are available. The online MBA option expands reach to working professionals across New England. Academic rigor is solid but the program doesn't have the depth of specialized concentrations found at larger schools.

Career Outcomes

New Hampshire's low-tax business environment attracts companies like DEKA Research, BAE Systems, and a growing tech community. Boston's employer base is accessible for internships and job searches. Average salary is approximately $67,000, reflecting the program's mix of New Hampshire and Greater Boston placement. Career services in a 30-person program provides individualized coaching. The smaller alumni network is tight-knit and responsive.

Who Should Apply

Career changers who need personal coaching through a career pivot. New Hampshire and New England residents who want to stay in the region. Students who prefer small cohorts over large classes. Applicants who value accessibility and personalized education.

What to Watch Out For

The $67K average salary is low by MBA standards. UNH's brand is regional; outside New England, it doesn't open doors the way a nationally ranked program does. Durham's isolation means limited on-site employer engagement. The 90% acceptance rate means less selectivity, which can dilute the peer learning experience. If you're targeting high-salary sectors like consulting, banking, or big tech, this program doesn't have the pipelines.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the acceptance rate at UNH Paul MBA?

UNH Paul admits approximately 90% of MBA applicants for the class of 2026. The program enrolls about 30 students, prioritizing accessibility and personalized education.

Is UNH good for New England careers?

UNH Paul's location provides access to New Hampshire's business-friendly economy and Boston's employer base (one hour south). The program is well-suited for professionals building careers in New England.

What is the average salary after UNH Paul MBA?

UNH Paul MBA graduates earn an average starting salary of approximately $67,000. Regional business, finance, and consulting roles in New England drive placement outcomes.
