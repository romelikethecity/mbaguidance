“$37K total tuition for a Jesuit MBA with analytics chops in Cincinnati's corporate corridor. Xavier Williams is quietly producing strong outcomes for a fraction of the attention.”

Program Overview

Xavier Williams operates in Cincinnati's corporate corridor, home to Procter & Gamble, Kroger, Fifth Third Bank, and Western & Southern Financial Group. That concentration of Fortune 500 headquarters in a mid-size city creates recruiting opportunities that the school's ranking understates. The Jesuit tradition adds an ethics dimension that surfaces in curriculum design and classroom culture. Business analytics and finance are the academic strengths.

The 32-student class gets personalized attention: faculty know every student, career services works with you individually, and the Jesuit emphasis on "cura personalis" (care for the whole person) translates to genuine investment in student success. At $37K total tuition for the full program, Xavier Williams is priced well below most private MBA programs. Test-optional admission opens the door for experienced professionals whose careers speak louder than standardized test scores.

Culture & Community

Xavier's Jesuit tradition creates a culture where ethics conversations are substantive, not performative. The 32-person class bonds through small cohort dynamics and Cincinnati's social scene. Cincinnati is an underrated city: affordable, culturally active (OTR neighborhood, Cincinnati Reds, food scene), and home to Fortune 500 companies that provide career stability. Students who want personal attention, values-centered education, and Midwest affordability find Xavier's culture appealing.

Academics & Curriculum

Business analytics courses are the academic highlight, with curriculum designed for the data-driven roles that Cincinnati's corporate employers are hiring for. Finance, marketing, and management round out the concentration options. The Jesuit approach to education means critical thinking and ethical reasoning are woven throughout coursework. Small class sizes enable discussion-based learning. Faculty are accessible and invested in student development beyond the classroom.

Career Outcomes

Cincinnati's Fortune 500 headquarters drive placement. P&G, Kroger, Fifth Third Bank, and Western & Southern are significant employers. Analytics (20%), finance (25%), marketing (20%), and operations (15%) are the top sectors. Average salary is approximately $88,000. The Xavier alumni network in Cincinnati is deeply established and opens doors in the local business community. Career services provide individual coaching in the 32-person program.

Who Should Apply

Analytics-minded professionals targeting Cincinnati's corporate employers. Candidates who value Jesuit ethics education alongside business fundamentals. Test-optional applicants with strong work experience. Anyone seeking a small-cohort MBA at a moderate price in a city with Fortune 500 career access.

What to Watch Out For

The Xavier Williams brand is local. Outside Cincinnati, the name requires context. The 32-person class means a very small annual alumni cohort. If your career goals point beyond Cincinnati and the Midwest, the program's primary network advantages don't travel well. The $88K average salary is solid for Cincinnati's cost of living but trails programs in higher-compensation markets. And test-optional admission, while accessible, means the incoming class may have wider variance in academic preparation.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the acceptance rate at Xavier Williams MBA?

Xavier Williams admits approximately 65% of MBA applicants for the class of 2026. The program enrolls about 32 students. Admission is test-optional.

Is Xavier Williams MBA test-optional?

Yes. Xavier Williams MBA does not require the GMAT or GRE for admission. Experienced professionals can apply based on work experience, undergraduate record, and professional achievements. This removes a barrier for candidates whose careers demonstrate more than a test score.

What is the average salary after Xavier Williams MBA?

Xavier Williams MBA graduates earn an average starting salary of approximately $88,000. Cincinnati-based employers including P&G, Kroger, and Fifth Third Bank drive the strongest placement outcomes.
