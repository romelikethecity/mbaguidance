Program Overview

Connecticut's economy runs on insurance, pharmaceuticals, and financial services. Quinnipiac's MBA program sits in the middle of that market with specializations in healthcare administration, analytics, and supply chain management. Hartford's insurance corridor, New Haven's biotech cluster, and the state's wealth management industry create an employer base that's concentrated and accessible from Hamden's suburban campus.

The 40-student class gets a program that balances core business skills with healthcare-specific coursework. At $26K per year, the pricing is moderate for a private New England university. Flexible scheduling (evening and weekend options) serves working professionals. Quinnipiac's growing reputation in healthcare education extends to the business school, where healthcare administration has become a signature strength.

Culture & Community

Quinnipiac's culture is collegial and professional. The 40-person class includes a mix of recent graduates and experienced professionals. The suburban campus in Hamden is comfortable and close to New Haven's food and culture scene. Connecticut's high quality of life and proximity to New York City (90 minutes by train) add lifestyle appeal. The healthcare administration students create a distinct community within the MBA, sharing career-specific networking and industry events.

Academics & Curriculum

Healthcare administration courses cover healthcare finance, policy, and operations management. Analytics and supply chain concentrations round out the specialization options. The core curriculum is standard MBA fare with a quantitative lean. Faculty bring both academic credentials and industry connections in insurance, pharmaceuticals, and healthcare delivery. The flexible format allows students to pace their progress over one to two years.

Career Outcomes

Connecticut's insurance headquarters (Aetna/CVS Health, Cigna, The Hartford), pharmaceutical companies, and hospital systems provide the primary recruiting pipeline. Healthcare administration, analytics, and finance roles drive placement. Average salary is approximately $85,000. The Connecticut alumni network is active in insurance and financial services. New York City's proximity adds career options for graduates willing to commute or relocate.

Who Should Apply

Healthcare professionals who want business credentials. Connecticut residents targeting local career advancement. Working professionals who need flexible scheduling. Anyone interested in the insurance or pharmaceutical industry.

What to Watch Out For

The Quinnipiac brand is strongest in Connecticut and southern New England. Outside the region, it competes with many mid-tier private universities for recognition. Connecticut's cost of living is high, and the $85K salary may feel tight in Fairfield County or Greater Hartford. If your career goals are in tech, startup, or West Coast industries, Quinnipiac's network doesn't extend to those markets.

What is the acceptance rate at Quinnipiac MBA?

Quinnipiac admits approximately 69% of MBA applicants for the class of 2026. The program enrolls about 40 students.

Is Quinnipiac good for healthcare management?

Quinnipiac's healthcare administration specialization connects to Connecticut's insurance and pharmaceutical corridor. The program's proximity to major healthcare employers (CVS Health/Aetna, Yale-New Haven Health) creates a pipeline for healthcare business careers.

What is the average salary after Quinnipiac MBA?

Quinnipiac MBA graduates earn an average starting salary of approximately $85,000. Healthcare administration, insurance, and finance roles in Connecticut drive placement outcomes.
