“A Jesuit MBA in the Gateway City. SLU Chaifetz connects to St. Louis's healthcare and corporate base with an ethical framework that runs deeper than a single course. The program is small, personal, and mission-driven.”

Program Overview

St. Louis punches above its weight as a corporate headquarters city. Centene, Emerson Electric, Edward Jones, and several major healthcare systems call it home. SLU Chaifetz's MBA connects to that corporate base with a Jesuit tradition that emphasizes ethical decision-making and service. The healthcare and supply chain concentrations align with the city's employer strengths.

The 30-student class gets individual attention from faculty who bridge academic research and St. Louis's business community. At $46K per year, the tuition carries a private university premium for a Midwest city. But the Jesuit network provides alumni connections that extend beyond St. Louis, and the program's ethical emphasis appeals to students who want their MBA to mean something beyond career advancement.

Culture & Community

SLU's campus sits in Midtown St. Louis, near the city's arts district and close to the Arch and downtown. The Jesuit culture shapes community life: service projects, ethics discussions, and a commitment to "the greater good" run through the MBA experience. The 30-person class is tight-knit. St. Louis is affordable, with excellent food, sports (Cardinals, Blues), and neighborhoods. Students who value community and purpose will find SLU's culture rewarding.

Academics & Curriculum

Ethics and social responsibility are integrated into core courses. Healthcare management draws on St. Louis's hospital systems and health sciences companies. Supply chain management connects to the city's logistics position (FedEx, manufacturing). Finance and marketing concentrations serve traditional career paths. Faculty bring Jesuit intellectual tradition and industry connections. The curriculum balances analytical rigor with values-driven leadership development.

Career Outcomes

St. Louis's corporate headquarters (Centene, Emerson Electric, Edward Jones), healthcare systems (BJC, SSM Health), and consulting firms provide the employer base. Average salary is approximately $76,000. The 81% employment rate reflects the regional market. The Jesuit alumni network connects graduates to employers who value the tradition. Career services is personalized in the small program. St. Louis's cost of living makes the $76K salary comfortable.

Who Should Apply

Midwest professionals who want to advance in St. Louis. Healthcare management candidates in the region. Leaders who value ethical foundations from the Jesuit tradition. Anyone drawn to a small, mission-driven MBA program.

What to Watch Out For

At $46K per year, SLU is expensive for a program in this tier. St. Louis's job market is concentrated in specific industries, and opportunities outside those sectors are limited. The brand is strongest in the Midwest; outside the region, SLU competes with many private universities for recognition. If you're targeting national firms or coastal markets, the network doesn't extend well.
