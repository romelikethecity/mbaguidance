Program Overview

Tampa Bay is one of the fastest-growing metro areas in the country. Financial services firms, healthcare companies, and tech startups are relocating at a pace that's reshaping the local economy. USF Muma's MBA sits in the middle of that growth at a price point that makes the ROI math simple. In-state tuition of $14K per year means the all-in cost is a fraction of what peer programs charge.

The 55-student class gets concentrations in cybersecurity, analytics, finance, and marketing. The cybersecurity track is distinctive: few MBA programs offer serious coursework in security management, and Tampa Bay's military and government presence (MacDill Air Force Base, CENTCOM) creates a unique employer pipeline for security-focused business leaders. The program's 40% acceptance rate makes it more selective than many schools ranked higher.

Culture & Community

USF Muma draws working professionals and career changers who chose Tampa Bay for the economics: low taxes, low cost of living, and a growing job market. The culture is practical and career-focused. The 55-person class includes a mix of Florida natives and transplants. Campus life centers on the Tampa campus, and the social scene revolves around study groups and professional networking events. Tampa's year-round warm weather and waterfront dining don't hurt quality of life.

Academics & Curriculum

Cybersecurity management courses are the academic headline. Analytics and data-driven decision making run through the core curriculum. Finance and marketing concentrations serve students targeting traditional business roles. The program partners with Tampa Bay companies for case competitions and consulting projects. Faculty bring research in analytics and information security. The curriculum balances breadth with modern specializations.

Career Outcomes

Tampa Bay's financial services sector (Raymond James, USAA, Citi) and healthcare companies (BayCare, Moffitt Cancer Center) are primary employers. Cybersecurity and government consulting roles draw on the MacDill AFB connection. Average salary is approximately $72,000. Tampa Bay's rapid growth means the employer base is expanding faster than the talent pool, which benefits graduates. Career services connects students to the regional network.

Who Should Apply

Florida residents who want maximum ROI from an MBA. Cybersecurity professionals seeking business leadership skills. Analytics-minded students who prefer a Sun Belt lifestyle. Career changers who need affordable tuition to make the math work.

What to Watch Out For

The $72K average salary is modest by MBA standards, though Tampa Bay's no-income-tax and low-cost-of-living offset much of the gap. The USF brand is regional: outside Florida, it competes for recognition with many mid-tier public universities. Out-of-state tuition jumps to $27K, which weakens the value proposition. If you're targeting NYC or Bay Area employers, USF's network doesn't reach those markets well.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the acceptance rate at USF Muma MBA?

USF Muma admits approximately 40% of MBA applicants for the class of 2026. The program enrolls about 55 students, making it more selective than many schools ranked above it.

Is USF Muma good for cybersecurity careers?

USF Muma's cybersecurity management concentration is distinctive among MBA programs. Tampa Bay's military presence (MacDill AFB, CENTCOM) and growing defense consulting sector create a pipeline for security-focused business leaders.

What is the average salary after USF Muma MBA?

USF Muma MBA graduates earn an average starting salary of approximately $72,000. Financial services, healthcare, and cybersecurity roles in Tampa Bay drive placement. The low in-state tuition ($14K/year) produces strong ROI despite the moderate salary figure.
