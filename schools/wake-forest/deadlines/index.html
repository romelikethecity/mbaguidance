Wake Forest Application Deadlines (2026)

Wake Forest offers three application rounds for the MBA class entering in fall 2026. Exact dates are published on the official Wake Forest website and may shift slightly year to year. Below are typical deadline windows based on historical patterns.

Which Round Should You Apply?

Round 1 is the optimal time to apply. Admissions committees review applications with the most available seats, the deepest scholarship pools, and fresh energy. Roughly 40-50% of the class is admitted in Round 1 at most programs.

Round 2 is still competitive. If your GMAT score, essays, or recommender letters aren't ready by Round 1, a strong Round 2 application is better than a rushed Round 1 submission. About 40% of admits come from Round 2.

Round 3 is viable for exceptional candidates only. Most seats are filled, and the bar is higher. Apply Round 3 only if your profile is unusually strong or if you're applying to programs ranked outside the top 15.

Key Application Components

  • GMAT or GRE score: See our GMAT vs GRE guide for choosing the right test.
  • Essays: See our Wake Forest essay tips for school-specific strategy.
  • 2 letters of recommendation: Choose people who know your work well. Direct supervisors are preferred.
  • Resume: One page, quantified achievements, no jargon.
  • Transcripts: Official transcripts from all undergraduate and graduate institutions.
  • Application fee: Typically $200-$275. Fee waivers are available for candidates demonstrating need or through campus visits.
