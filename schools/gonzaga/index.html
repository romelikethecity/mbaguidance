Program Overview

Gonzaga is best known for basketball, but the business school produces outcomes that deserve their own attention. A $101K average starting salary from a program in Spokane, Washington raises eyebrows. The secret is the Jesuit network: Gonzaga graduates join an alumni community that spans the Pacific Northwest's major employers and values hiring from within the Jesuit tradition. The 30-student class gets an education that's personal in ways that larger programs can't replicate.

Spokane is not Seattle or Portland, but that's part of the value proposition. The cost of living is lower, the campus is beautiful, and the program's intimacy means every faculty member is invested in every student's outcome. At $41K per year, the tuition is mid-range for private universities. The salary outcomes suggest the investment pays off, particularly for students who stay in the Pacific Northwest.

Culture & Community

Gonzaga's Jesuit tradition shapes the MBA culture: service, ethics, and the concept of "cura personalis" (care for the whole person). The 30-person class bonds tightly in Spokane's small-city environment. The university's basketball program creates campus energy that's outsized for Gonzaga's enrollment. Students who want a values-driven education in a close-knit community will find Gonzaga's culture rewarding. Students who want big-city networking and social scenes should look at Seattle or Portland programs.

Academics & Curriculum

The curriculum covers business fundamentals with an emphasis on ethical decision-making and leadership. Finance and accounting concentrations are available. The small class size allows faculty to tailor instruction to cohort needs. The Jesuit tradition of intellectual rigor runs through the academic experience. No specialized tech or analytics concentrations, but the foundational business education is thorough and well-taught.

Career Outcomes

Pacific Northwest employers in healthcare (Providence, MultiCare), technology (Amazon, Microsoft offices in the region), and financial services provide the career pipeline. Average salary is approximately $101,000. The 85% employment rate is strong for this tier. The Jesuit alumni network opens doors at employers who value the tradition's ethical emphasis. Career services provides individual coaching in the small program. Graduates who stay in the Pacific Northwest see the strongest outcomes.

Who Should Apply

Pacific Northwest professionals who want to advance in the region. Leaders who value ethical foundations in their business education. Students who prefer a 30-person cohort to a 200-person class. Anyone attracted to the Jesuit approach to business leadership.

What to Watch Out For

Spokane is not Seattle. The local employer base is limited, and most graduates target jobs elsewhere in the Pacific Northwest. At $41K per year, the tuition is a premium for a program outside major rankings. The Gonzaga MBA brand carries weight regionally but is less recognized nationally outside Jesuit circles. If you're targeting finance hubs, tech centers, or consulting firms outside the Pacific Northwest, the network thins quickly.
