Program Overview

Marquette's MBA offers 25+ concentrations, which is an extraordinary number for a 30-person class. Sports business, healthcare administration, supply chain and logistics, real estate, and not-for-profit management sit alongside traditional tracks in finance, marketing, and management. That breadth means you can build a customized MBA. The Jesuit tradition adds an ethics dimension and a community orientation that shapes classroom culture.

Milwaukee is the backdrop: home to Northwestern Mutual, Rockwell Automation, Harley-Davidson, and a growing healthcare sector. The 12:1 student-faculty ratio means individualized attention. Class sizes of 15-30 students create discussion-based learning environments. The $47K total tuition for the program is moderate for a Jesuit university, and Milwaukee's cost of living is among the lowest of any major metro with a ranked MBA program.

Culture & Community

Marquette's Jesuit culture emphasizes service, ethics, and community. Milwaukee is a blue-collar city with big-city amenities: professional sports (Bucks, Brewers), a lakefront, craft breweries, and a growing food scene. The 30-person class bonds through shared academic intensity and Milwaukee's manageable social landscape. Students who want a values-centered MBA in a livable, affordable city will find Marquette's environment grounding.

Academics & Curriculum

The concentration breadth is the academic identity. Sports business courses connect to Wisconsin's professional and collegiate athletics infrastructure. Healthcare administration draws on Milwaukee's major hospital systems. Supply chain and logistics connect to Wisconsin's manufacturing economy. Real estate and finance round out the traditional options. The Jesuit approach means ethical reasoning appears across courses, not in a standalone elective. Faculty in the small program are accessible and mentorship-oriented.

Career Outcomes

Milwaukee employers drive placement. Northwestern Mutual, Rockwell Automation, GE Healthcare, and regional hospital systems are significant recruiters. Finance (20%), healthcare (18%), marketing (15%), and supply chain (12%) are the top sectors. Average salary is approximately $91,000. Milwaukee's corporate community is tight-knit, and the Marquette alumni network in Wisconsin provides access that larger, less connected programs can't match. Sports business graduates find roles across the Midwest's professional and collegiate athletics organizations.

Who Should Apply

Professionals targeting sports business, healthcare administration, or Milwaukee's corporate employers. Candidates who want concentration variety in a small, personalized program. Jesuit education values matter to you. Anyone seeking Midwest career access at a livable, affordable city with a strong alumni community.

What to Watch Out For

Milwaukee is a smaller market than Chicago, Minneapolis, or other Midwest hubs. The Marquette MBA brand, while respected in Wisconsin, has limited national reach. The $47K total tuition is high for a program at this ranking level. The 30-person class means a small graduating cohort each year. If your career goals are in coastal markets or require a nationally recognized MBA brand, Marquette's geographic and brand limitations apply. And 25+ concentrations in a 30-person class means some specialties have very few students, which limits peer learning in those tracks.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the acceptance rate at Marquette MBA?

Marquette admits approximately 69% of MBA applicants for the class of 2026. The program enrolls about 30 students with a 12:1 student-faculty ratio.

Is Marquette good for sports business?

Marquette's sports business concentration connects to Wisconsin's professional and collegiate athletics infrastructure. The Jesuit university's location in Milwaukee, home to the Bucks and Brewers, provides networking and career opportunities in the sports industry.

What is the average salary after Marquette MBA?

Marquette MBA graduates earn an average starting salary of approximately $91,000. Milwaukee employers in finance, healthcare, marketing, and supply chain drive placement.
