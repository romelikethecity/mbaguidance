Program Overview

Pittsburgh used to be a steel city. Now it's a healthcare, tech, and energy transition city. Duquesne's MBA program sits at that intersection, with a sustainability concentration that connects to the region's reinvention story. The one-year format is the headline: you're in and out in 12 months, which cuts opportunity cost in half compared to traditional two-year programs.

The Spiritan Catholic tradition emphasizes service and ethics. It shows up in coursework on stakeholder management and corporate responsibility. The 35-student class gets individual attention from faculty who bridge academic research and industry practice. At $31K per year (which is the total cost for the one-year format), the price is moderate for a private university. Pittsburgh's affordable cost of living stretches the budget further.

Culture & Community

Duquesne's hilltop campus overlooks downtown Pittsburgh and the confluence of three rivers. The MBA culture blends Spiritan values (service, ethics, community) with Pittsburgh's pragmatic work ethic. The 35-person class bonds quickly in the accelerated timeline. Students tend to be professionals with 3-6 years of experience who want to advance without spending two years in school. Pittsburgh's growing food scene, sports culture, and affordable housing make it an underrated MBA city.

Academics & Curriculum

The sustainability MBA is the academic differentiator. Courses in environmental management, corporate social responsibility, and sustainable operations are woven into the core. Finance, supply chain, and consulting concentrations serve students targeting traditional business roles. The one-year format is intense: 11 months of coursework with no summer break. Faculty are accessible, and the small class size means every student participates in every discussion.

Career Outcomes

Pittsburgh's healthcare giants (UPMC, Highmark), financial services (PNC, BNY Mellon), and energy companies provide the employer base. Sustainability roles in energy transition, consulting, and corporate responsibility are growing. Average salary is approximately $77,000. The one-year format means you lose less income while in school. Pittsburgh's alumni network is strong locally, and the city's tech growth (Google, Meta, Uber all have Pittsburgh offices) is expanding the opportunity set.

Who Should Apply

Professionals who want an MBA in 12 months. Sustainability-focused candidates who want coursework that matches their values. Anyone drawn to Pittsburgh's affordable, high-quality-of-life market. Candidates who value the ethical grounding of a Spiritan education.

What to Watch Out For

The one-year format leaves no room for a summer internship, which matters for career changers who need a trial run in their target industry. The Duquesne brand is strongest in Pittsburgh and the tri-state area. The $77K salary is moderate, and if you're paying full tuition at a private university, the ROI requires careful calculation. Sustainability roles can be competitive and lower-paying than traditional business functions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the acceptance rate at Duquesne Palumbo-Donahue MBA?

Duquesne Palumbo-Donahue admits approximately 72% of MBA applicants for the class of 2026. The program enrolls about 35 students.

Is Duquesne's sustainability MBA worth it?

Duquesne's sustainability MBA is one of the few one-year programs with a dedicated sustainability focus. If your career goals involve corporate responsibility, environmental management, or ESG, the specialized curriculum provides coursework that general MBA programs lack.

What is the average salary after Duquesne MBA?

Duquesne MBA graduates earn an average starting salary of approximately $77,000. Healthcare, financial services, and sustainability roles in Pittsburgh drive placement. The one-year format reduces total cost and opportunity cost.
