“A Jesuit MBA in America's third-largest business market. Loyola Chicago Quinlan offers Chicago corporate access with an ethical foundation that distinguishes graduates in leadership conversations.”

Program Overview

Chicago is home to Boeing, McDonald's, Abbott, Walgreens, and dozens of other Fortune 500 companies. Loyola Chicago Quinlan's MBA program taps into that corporate density from its Water Tower campus on the Magnificent Mile. The Jesuit tradition adds an ethics and social responsibility dimension that runs through the curriculum. It's the kind of program where "stakeholder capitalism" isn't a trend but a teaching philosophy.

The 40-student class gets a program that balances core business education with healthcare management and supply chain concentrations. At $46K per year, the tuition carries a private university premium. But Chicago's job market and the Quinlan alumni network in the city create career access that justifies the investment for professionals who plan to build their careers in the Midwest's largest metro.

Culture & Community

Loyola Chicago's Water Tower campus puts students in the heart of downtown Chicago. The culture blends Jesuit values (ethics, service, "men and women for others") with Chicago's blue-collar work ethic. The 40-person class is diverse and practical. Students are there to advance their careers, and the Jesuit framework adds a moral dimension to business decisions. The social scene benefits from Chicago's world-class restaurants, music, and sports.

Academics & Curriculum

Ethics and social responsibility courses are woven into the core curriculum. Healthcare management courses build on Chicago's massive hospital and pharmaceutical industry. Supply chain management connects to the city's logistics infrastructure (O'Hare Airport, railroad hub). Finance and marketing concentrations serve traditional career paths. Faculty bridge academic research and Chicago's corporate community.

Career Outcomes

Chicago's corporate headquarters provide the recruiting pipeline. Healthcare companies (Abbott, Baxter, Walgreens), consulting firms, and financial services companies are top employers. Average salary is approximately $92,000. The Quinlan alumni network in Chicago is loyal and growing. Career services focuses on connecting students to the city's employer base. For professionals who plan to stay in Chicago, the access is strong.

Who Should Apply

Chicago-based professionals who want to advance locally. Healthcare management candidates targeting the city's hospital and pharmaceutical sector. Leaders who value ethical foundations in business education. Anyone who wants a Jesuit MBA in a world-class city.

What to Watch Out For

Loyola Chicago competes with Booth, Kellogg, and multiple other Chicago MBA programs for employer attention. At $46K per year, the tuition is high for a program outside the top 50. The brand is strongest in Chicago; outside the Midwest, Quinlan doesn't carry the same weight. If your career goals require a nationally recognized MBA brand, the ROI calculation gets harder.
