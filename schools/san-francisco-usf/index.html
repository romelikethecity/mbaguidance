“A Jesuit MBA in San Francisco with the Bay Area's tech and startup ecosystem on the doorstep. USF's social responsibility emphasis and entrepreneurship resources create a program for mission-driven business builders in one of the world's most dynamic markets.”

Program Overview

San Francisco is one of the world's great business cities: tech headquarters, venture capital, fintech, and a startup culture that permeates everything. USF's MBA sits on a hilltop campus overlooking the Golden Gate Bridge, putting students in a city where innovation is the primary industry. The Jesuit tradition adds social responsibility and ethical leadership to the business education, which resonates in a city that debates tech ethics as regularly as it debates housing policy.

The 30-student class gets entrepreneurship resources, Bay Area employer access, and a Jesuit education at $40K per year. That's expensive for this tier, but the San Francisco location provides access to an employer ecosystem that most programs can't match. The program is small and personal, with faculty who know every student. The Jesuit network extends globally, connecting USF graduates to a community that values both competence and conscience.

Culture & Community

USF's campus is in the Inner Richmond, a residential San Francisco neighborhood with views of the Golden Gate Bridge and easy access to the city's tech corridors. The Jesuit culture emphasizes social justice, ethics, and community service. The 30-person class includes international students, tech professionals, and social entrepreneurs. The vibe is San Francisco: progressive, globally-minded, and curious. Students who want a values-driven MBA in a world-class city will find the culture stimulating.

Academics & Curriculum

Entrepreneurship courses provide startup methodology and incubator access. Social responsibility and ethics are woven into the core curriculum. Finance and marketing concentrations serve traditional career paths. The Bay Area's tech and startup community provides guest speakers, mentors, and project partners. Faculty bring both Jesuit intellectual tradition and Bay Area industry connections. The curriculum balances practical business skills with mission-driven leadership development.

Career Outcomes

Bay Area tech companies, startups, financial services firms, and social enterprises provide the employer base. Average salary is approximately $83,000. The 80% employment rate reflects San Francisco's competitive job market. Career services connects students to the local ecosystem. The Jesuit alumni network provides connections in social enterprise and mission-driven organizations globally. For social entrepreneurs, the Bay Area ecosystem and USF's mission create a supportive launch pad.

Who Should Apply

Social entrepreneurs who want a Bay Area launch pad. Mission-driven business builders who value Jesuit ethics. Bay Area professionals who want to advance locally. Anyone attracted to San Francisco's tech ecosystem with a values-driven education.

What to Watch Out For

At $40K per year, USF is expensive for a program in this tier. The $83K salary is modest for San Francisco's high cost of living. The USF brand competes with Stanford GSB, Haas, and Santa Clara in the Bay Area. Outside the Jesuit network and San Francisco, the brand is less recognized. If you're targeting pure corporate careers in finance, consulting, or tech, more established programs offer stronger pipelines. And San Francisco's cost of living may require taking on significant debt.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the acceptance rate at USF School of Management MBA?

USF admits approximately 65% of MBA applicants for the class of 2026. The program enrolls about 30 students.

Is USF good for social enterprise?

USF's Jesuit tradition and San Francisco location create a strong environment for social enterprise and mission-driven business. The program's emphasis on social responsibility, combined with Bay Area startup resources, supports founders building businesses with social impact.

What is the average salary after USF MBA?

USF MBA graduates earn an average starting salary of approximately $83,000. Tech, social enterprise, finance, and consulting roles in the Bay Area drive placement.
