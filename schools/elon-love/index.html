Program Overview

Elon University has built a national reputation for teaching quality. US News consistently ranks it among the top schools for undergraduate teaching, and that emphasis carries into the MBA program. The Love School's approach is hands-on: consulting projects with real companies, study abroad options, and a faculty culture where professors are mentors, not just instructors.

The 30-student class gets a program in the Piedmont Triad region of North Carolina, between Raleigh-Durham's Research Triangle and Charlotte's financial center. At $22K per year, the tuition is moderate for a private school. The experiential learning model means students graduate with project portfolios and professional relationships, not just transcripts. Faculty investment in student outcomes is Elon's distinguishing characteristic.

Culture & Community

Elon's campus is beautiful, frequently photographed, and designed around a 636-acre campus with brick walkways and oak trees. The culture is engaged and community-oriented. The 30-person MBA class joins a university known for student engagement and faculty accessibility. The small-town setting in central North Carolina is comfortable but quiet. Students who want the campus experience and personal attention will find Elon rewarding. Students who need big-city energy should look elsewhere.

Academics & Curriculum

Experiential learning is the academic philosophy. Consulting projects with regional companies provide applied experience. Study abroad options add international perspective. Core courses cover business fundamentals with an emphasis on practical application. Faculty are selected for teaching ability, not just research output. The curriculum integrates teamwork and presentation skills throughout. Broad concentration options serve diverse career goals.

Career Outcomes

North Carolina employers in healthcare, financial services, manufacturing, and consulting provide the career pipeline. The Research Triangle (Raleigh-Durham) and Charlotte's financial center are both accessible. Average salary is approximately $83,000. The 84% employment rate reflects the program's career focus. Career services provides individual coaching and employer connections. The Elon alumni network, while smaller than state universities, is engaged and supportive.

Who Should Apply

Students who value teaching quality and faculty mentoring. Hands-on learners who prefer projects to lectures. North Carolina professionals targeting regional career advancement. Anyone drawn to the intimate, campus-based MBA experience.

What to Watch Out For

Elon is a small private university, and the MBA brand is limited outside North Carolina and Elon's alumni circles. The $83K salary is moderate, and the small-town location means limited local employer access. If you're targeting major metro areas or industries concentrated outside the Southeast, Elon's network is thin. The $22K tuition is moderate but not cheap for a regional program.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the acceptance rate at Elon Love MBA?

Elon Love admits approximately 70% of MBA applicants for the class of 2026. The program enrolls about 30 students.

What makes Elon MBA different from other programs?

Elon's MBA emphasizes experiential learning and faculty mentoring. The university's reputation for teaching quality extends to the business school. Students work on real consulting projects, have access to study abroad, and receive personalized career guidance from faculty mentors.

What is the average salary after Elon Love MBA?

Elon Love MBA graduates earn an average starting salary of approximately $83,000. Healthcare, financial services, and consulting roles in North Carolina drive placement.
