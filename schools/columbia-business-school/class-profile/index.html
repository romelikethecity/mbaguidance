Columbia Business School's class of 850 students represents a large community. A larger class means more diverse perspectives, broader recruiting connections, and a wider alumni network.

Academic Profile

The average GMAT score is 729, with the middle 80% typically ranging from 699 to 749. The average GPA is 3.6. These are averages, meaning roughly half the admitted class scored below these numbers. Strong work experience, compelling essays, and demonstrated leadership can offset below-average test scores.

Most admitted students have 3-7 years of work experience, with an average of approximately 5 years. The admissions committee looks for candidates who've had enough professional experience to contribute meaningfully to classroom discussions but are early enough in their careers to benefit from the MBA's career-changing potential.

Professional Background

Columbia Business School attracts students from diverse professional backgrounds. Common pre-MBA industries include consulting, financial services, technology, and non-profit/government. The school particularly values candidates whose backgrounds align with its strengths in Finance, Media, Real Estate, Value Investing.

Diversity of background is a deliberate admissions strategy. Schools build classes where a former investment banker sits next to a Peace Corps volunteer sits next to a software engineer. This diversity is the source of the MBA classroom's value.

What This Means for Applicants

Understanding the class profile helps you position your application. If your GMAT is below 729, consider retaking or emphasizing other strengths. If you come from an over-represented background (consulting, banking), you'll need to differentiate on leadership, personal story, and school fit. If you come from an under-represented background (military, non-profit, healthcare), that diversity is an asset.

The class profile also tells you what your learning environment will look like. A class of 850 with these academic credentials means rigorous, fast-paced discussions where intellectual humility serves you well.
