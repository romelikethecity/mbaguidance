How JPMorgan hires MBAs

JPMorgan runs one of the largest MBA Associate programs in banking, hiring 350-450 MBAs annually across Investment Banking, Markets, and Asset Management. The firm's scale and product breadth give MBAs more cross-divisional optionality than the smaller bulge brackets.

JPM recruits at every M7 plus top-15 program with on-campus interviews. The Investment Banking division and the Markets (sales & trading) division each run separate recruiting funnels. Internships are the primary path to full-time offers.

Interview process

Standard banking interview format: behavioral, technicals (valuation, accounting, M&A), and a fit interview. JPM's behavioral screen often includes a stress component to assess composure under pressure.

JPMorgan compensation breakdown

First-year total compensation at JPMorgan runs about $225K for US-based MBA hires. The breakdown: $175K base, $50K signing bonus paid at start, $0K performance bonus or stock vest in the first year.

Year-two compensation typically grows 10-15%, with promotion to Senior Associate or equivalent in years 2-3 producing larger jumps. Top performers in Investment Banking can clear $400K total comp by year 4-5.

What MBA programs does JPMorgan hire from?

JPMorgan recruits hardest at Wharton, Harvard Business School, and Columbia Business School, with significant on-campus presence at the rest of the M7 plus the top 15. Off-campus hires from outside this list are possible but require deeper networking and stronger backgrounds.

What's the starting salary at JPMorgan for MBAs?

First-year compensation at JPMorgan runs $175K base, $50K signing bonus, and $0K performance bonus, totaling roughly $225K. RSU grants and other variable compensation can shift the total significantly for tech firms.

How hard is it to get hired by JPMorgan after an MBA?

JPMorgan is a Bulge Bracket firm in Investment Banking, which puts it in the top tier of MBA selectivity. Even at target schools, only 10-30% of applicants typically receive offers. Most successful candidates come in with relevant pre-MBA experience and strong campus interview performance.

What's the JPMorgan interview process like?

Standard banking interview format: behavioral, technicals (valuation, accounting, M&A), and a fit interview. JPM's behavioral screen often includes a stress component to assess composure under pressure.
