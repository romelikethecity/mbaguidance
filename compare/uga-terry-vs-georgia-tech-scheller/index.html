Why People Compare These Two

Terry (#51) and Scheller (#26) are Georgia's two flagship MBA programs. The 25-spot ranking gap favors Georgia Tech, but UGA's loyal alumni network and Athens campus create a different kind of value. The comparison often comes down to Athens vs Atlanta and whether you want a tech-MBA hybrid or a traditional business education.

Academics and Approach

Scheller integrates Georgia Tech's engineering and computing strengths into its MBA, with deep analytics and operations curriculum. Terry offers a traditional MBA with specializations in real estate, risk management, and marketing. Terry's curriculum is broader; Scheller's is more quantitative and tech-focused.

The Honest Take

Georgia Tech's higher ranking and Atlanta location provide better career access for most paths. UGA Terry is the pick for candidates who want the SEC campus experience, strong UGA alumni connections in the Southeast, or who receive significantly better scholarship offers. For tech and analytics careers, Scheller is the clear winner.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the ranking gap significant?

The 25-spot gap (#26 vs #51) matters for competitive national recruiting. For Georgia-based careers, both schools are well-known and respected. Georgia Tech's brand carries further nationally.

Which has more school spirit?

UGA's Bulldog culture and SEC athletics create a campus experience that's hard to match. Georgia Tech has strong engineering culture but a different social vibe. If campus culture matters, UGA delivers more of a traditional college experience.

Which is more affordable?

Similar in-state tuition as both are public universities. Athens cost of living is significantly lower than Atlanta. For total cost, UGA is typically cheaper.
