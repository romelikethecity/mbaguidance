Why People Compare These Two

Both are small-town MBA programs with outstanding ROI. Kansas State ($8K/year) is the cheapest AACSB-accredited MBA. Mississippi State ($12.3K/year) produces $92K salaries from a 22-person class. Both are in college towns with limited local employers. The comparison is about value maximization in different regions.

The Honest Take

Kansas State wins on tuition ($8K vs $12.3K). Mississippi State wins on salary outcomes ($92K vs $78K). Both produce exceptional ROI. Kansas State is the better choice for agribusiness careers and minimum financial risk. Mississippi State is the better choice for defense-connected careers and students who want the most intimate class size in MBA education.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is the cheapest?

Kansas State ($8K/year in-state) is the cheapest AACSB MBA in the country. Mississippi State ($12.3K/year) is also extremely affordable.

Which has better salaries?

Mississippi State ($92K) outearns Kansas State ($78K). The gap may reflect Mississippi State's defense and engineering connections.

Which has a smaller class?

Mississippi State enrolls about 22 students per class. Kansas State enrolls about 53. Mississippi State is the more intimate program.
