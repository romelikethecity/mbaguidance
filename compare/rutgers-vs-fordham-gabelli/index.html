Why People Compare These Two

Rutgers (#55) and Fordham Gabelli (#58) both serve the NYC metro area from different angles. Rutgers is in Newark, NJ, with a public university cost structure and strong pharma connections (New Jersey is the pharmaceutical capital of the US). Fordham is in Manhattan with direct access to NYC employers and a Jesuit educational tradition.

Career Markets

Rutgers has strong pipelines into pharmaceutical companies (Johnson & Johnson, Merck, Pfizer), New Jersey financial services, and supply chain roles. Fordham Gabelli places well into NYC financial services, media, and marketing. Both recruit in the broader NYC metro area, but the employer specializations differ.

The Honest Take

If you want pharma, healthcare, or NJ-based corporate careers, Rutgers is the obvious pick with better value. If you want to be physically in Manhattan, recruit into NYC financial services, or value Fordham's Lincoln Center campus, Gabelli provides that. Similar rankings mean the decision is about location and industry preference.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is more affordable?

Rutgers (in-state) is significantly cheaper as a public university. Fordham is a private university with higher tuition. For NJ residents, Rutgers's value is compelling.

Which is better for pharma careers?

Rutgers. New Jersey's pharmaceutical industry concentration gives Rutgers MBA graduates direct access to J&J, Merck, Pfizer, and dozens of biotech companies. No other NYC-area MBA matches this.

Which has better NYC access?

Fordham's Manhattan campus provides easier physical access to NYC employers. Rutgers in Newark is a short PATH train ride from Manhattan but requires a commute. For daily NYC immersion, Fordham wins.
