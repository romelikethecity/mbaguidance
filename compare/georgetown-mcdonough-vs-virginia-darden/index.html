Why People Compare These Two

McDonough (#20) and Darden (#14) serve the Mid-Atlantic and attract candidates who want to stay in the DC-Virginia corridor. Georgetown's DC location provides unique access to government and policy-adjacent careers. Darden's Charlottesville campus delivers the classic residential MBA experience with the case method.

Career Focus

Georgetown owns government consulting (Booz Allen, Deloitte Federal, Accenture Federal) and provides access to the World Bank, IMF, and policy organizations that no other MBA program can match. Darden places heavily into traditional consulting and increasingly into tech. Both recruit nationally, but the local career ecosystems are completely different.

Teaching Method

Darden uses the case method almost exclusively, second only to HBS in its commitment to case-based learning. Georgetown uses a mix of cases, lectures, and experiential learning. If the case method appeals to you (daily cold calls, structured debate, learning through discussion), Darden's approach is a clear differentiator.

The Honest Take

If you want to work in or around government (consulting, policy, international development), Georgetown is the only MBA that competes. If you want the case method, a residential community, and strong national consulting placement, Darden delivers that from a lower cost-of-living location. Darden's 6-spot ranking advantage matters for competitive roles.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is better for consulting?

Darden places more graduates into traditional consulting (MBB, Big 4). Georgetown dominates government and federal consulting. If your target is Booz Allen or Deloitte Federal, Georgetown is the pick. For McKinsey or BCG, Darden has a stronger pipeline.

Which has a better location?

Depends on what you want. DC is a world-class city with museums, restaurants, and professional networking opportunities everywhere. Charlottesville is a charming college town with lower living costs and a focused academic environment. Both are excellent places to spend two years.

Is Darden's ranking advantage significant?

The gap (#14 vs #20) matters for careers where top-15 status is a filter. For government consulting, policy, and DC-area careers, Georgetown's location advantage outweighs Darden's ranking. For national recruiting, Darden's brand carries slightly further.
