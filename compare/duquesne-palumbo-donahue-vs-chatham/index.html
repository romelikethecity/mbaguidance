Why People Compare These Two

Both are small private university MBAs in Pittsburgh competing below Tepper (Carnegie Mellon). Duquesne's one-year format and sustainability focus provide a distinct identity. Chatham's women's leadership heritage and lower cost appeal to different candidates. Both serve Pittsburgh's corporate base.

The Honest Take

Duquesne has the stronger brand and better outcomes ($77K vs $65K salary). The one-year format saves time and opportunity cost. Chatham's lower tuition ($16.5K vs $31K) and women's leadership tradition appeal to specific candidates. For most Pittsburgh professionals, Duquesne is the better investment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is faster?

Duquesne's one-year format finishes in 12 months. Chatham's program takes 1-2 years depending on pace.

Which is more affordable?

Chatham ($16.5K/year) is roughly half of Duquesne ($31K for the one-year program). Chatham is the budget choice.

Which has better outcomes?

Duquesne produces higher salaries ($77K vs $65K) and has a stronger brand in Pittsburgh. Chatham appeals for its women's leadership focus and flexibility.
